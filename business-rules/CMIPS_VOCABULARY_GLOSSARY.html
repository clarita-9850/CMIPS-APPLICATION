Comprehensive Terminology Reference for IHSS Case Management Information Payroll System

About This Glossary

Purpose: Comprehensive reference of all terms, acronyms, and concepts used in CMIPS (Case Management Information Payroll System) for IHSS (In-Home Supportive Services).

Source: Compiled from DSD Sections 20 (Intake), 21 (Service Eligibility), 22 (Final Determination), 23 (Provider Management), 24 (Time & Attendance), 25 (Case Maintenance), and 28 (Reporting).

Audience: Caseworkers, supervisors, trainers, administrators, providers, and anyone working with CMIPS.

DSD Sections Covered

A
Accompanying to Medical Appointments
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Assistance with transportation to and from medical appointments, including accompanying the recipient inside medical facilities, waiting during appointments, and helping with communication with medical staff.
Aid Code
MEDS Term
A code in the Medi-Cal system that identifies the type of Medi-Cal eligibility a recipient has. Examples include aid codes for SSI-linked Medi-Cal, Aged/Disabled programs, or other eligibility categories. The aid code determines the funding source for IHSS services.
Aid Paid Pending
Appeal Rights
The right of a recipient to continue receiving IHSS services during a state fair hearing appeal if the recipient files the appeal within 10 days of receiving an adverse Notice of Action and requests continuation of services.
Application
Case Status
A formal request for IHSS services submitted by or on behalf of an individual who needs in-home assistance. The application (form SOC 873) triggers a 45-day processing timeline during which the county must complete an assessment and make an eligibility determination.
Application Date
Timeline
The date when an IHSS application is officially filed. This date starts the 45-day federal processing clock and establishes the potential benefit effective date if the application is approved.
Assessment
Core Function
A comprehensive in-home evaluation conducted by a caseworker to determine an applicant's or recipient's functional abilities and need for IHSS services. The assessment includes evaluating the person's ability to perform activities of daily living and instrumental activities of daily living across eight service categories.
Types: Initial Assessment, Reassessment, Change Assessment
Authorization
Approval
The official approval of IHSS services specifying the number of hours and types of services a recipient is eligible to receive. An authorization establishes the authorization period (typically 12 months) and the monthly service hours.
Authorization Period
Timeline
The full date range for which a case is authorized to receive IHSS services, typically 12 months. The authorization period runs from the authorization start date (usually the assessment date) to the authorization end date.
Authorization Segment
Timeline
A division within an authorization period representing a distinct level of authorized hours. When a recipient's hours change mid-authorization (through a change assessment), a new segment is created with different authorized hours.
Example: A recipient is authorized 100 hours/month from January-June (Segment 1), then hours increase to 140 hours/month from July-December (Segment 2) within the same 12-month authorization period.
B
Bowel and Bladder Care
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Assistance with toileting needs, including getting to and from the toilet, transferring on/off toilet, managing clothing, cleaning after elimination, catheter care, and ostomy care.
Business Rules
Validation
Automated rules programmed into CMIPS that validate data entry, calculate authorized hours based on functional rankings, determine eligibility, and enforce policy requirements. Business rules ensure consistency and compliance across all cases.
C
Case
Core Record
An active IHSS record for a recipient who has been approved for services. A case includes all information about the recipient, authorized services, providers, timesheets, and payments.
Case Home
CMIPS Screen
The central screen in CMIPS for a specific case, providing access to all case-related information including assessments, authorizations, providers, timesheets, payments, and case notes.
Case Owner
Role
The caseworker or social worker assigned primary responsibility for a case. The case owner conducts assessments, makes eligibility determinations, manages providers, and handles ongoing case maintenance.
Case Service Month
Payment Record
A CMIPS-generated record for each calendar month within an authorization segment. The Case Service Month record contains the authorized hours for that specific month and is used to validate timesheets and process payments.
CC
Contract Care Mode of Service
Contract Care mode of service where IHSS services are provided by a county-contracted agency rather than an individual provider. Used when the recipient cannot be the employer of record or requires agency oversight.
See also: IP (Individual Provider), HM (Homemaker)
CDSS
California Department of Social Services State Agency
The state agency that oversees the IHSS program in California. CDSS establishes program regulations, provides guidance to counties, and administers state-level program functions.
Change Assessment
Assessment Type
An assessment conducted mid-authorization when a recipient's condition or circumstances change significantly. A change assessment can result in increased or decreased hours without waiting for the annual reassessment.
Check Eligibility
CMIPS Function
A CMIPS function that allows caseworkers to preview the eligibility determination outcome before officially submitting a case for approval. Check Eligibility runs business rules and shows what would be authorized without generating a Notice of Action.
CIN
Client Index Number Identifier
A unique nine-character identifier (eight digits plus one letter) assigned to each person in California's public assistance system by the Statewide Client Index (SCI). The CIN ensures a person has only one record across all programs and counties.
Example CIN: 12345678A
CIN Clearance
System Process
The process of searching the Statewide Client Index (SCI) to find an existing CIN or obtain a new CIN when creating a case. CIN clearance prevents duplicate person records and ensures proper identification across programs.
CMIPS
Case Management Information Payroll System Core System
The statewide computer system used by California counties to manage IHSS cases, process provider enrollments, authorize services, approve timesheets, and process payroll. CMIPS integrates case management and payroll functions in one system.
CORI
Criminal Offender Record Information Background Check
A criminal background check conducted on all IHSS provider applicants to identify disqualifying convictions. CORI checks reveal Tier 1 convictions (permanently disqualifying, no waiver) and Tier 2 convictions (disqualifying unless recipient waives).
D
Disaster Preparedness
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories (though rarely authorized). Planning and preparation to help the recipient respond to emergencies such as fires, earthquakes, or other disasters.
Domestic Services
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Household tasks including meal preparation and cleanup, laundry, routine household cleaning, and food shopping. These services help the recipient maintain a safe and sanitary living environment.
DSD
Detailed System Design Technical Documentation
Comprehensive technical documentation that describes how CMIPS functions work, including business processes, screen designs, business rules, error messages, and system interfaces. The DSD is organized into numbered sections covering different functional areas.
E
Eligibility Worker
Role
A county staff member who determines eligibility for public assistance programs including IHSS. May conduct intake, process applications, or perform specialized eligibility functions.
ESP
Electronic Services Portal Provider System
A web-based portal where IHSS providers can submit timesheets electronically, view payment history, update personal information, enroll in direct deposit, and communicate with the county. ESP provides 24/7 access for providers.
EVV
Electronic Visit Verification Compliance System
A federally-required system that electronically captures when IHSS services begin and end. Providers use mobile apps, fixed devices, telephone, or web portals to "clock in" and "clock out" at the recipient's home. EVV data must include: service type, recipient, date, location, provider, and start/end times.
F
Final Determination
Decision Process
The process of making the final eligibility and authorization decision on an IHSS application or reassessment. Final determination includes checking eligibility, submitting for approval, and establishing the authorization.
FLSA
Fair Labor Standards Act Federal Law
Federal law establishing minimum wage, overtime pay, recordkeeping, and child labor standards. IHSS providers are covered by FLSA and must be paid overtime (1.5x regular rate) for hours worked over 40 in their defined workweek.
Functional Index
Assessment Tool
A standardized ranking system (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or N/A) used to evaluate a recipient's functional ability to perform specific tasks within each service category. Rankings guide the determination of authorized hours.
1 = Unable/Unable to Participate
2 = Needs Assistance
3 = Needs Supervision
4 = Independent with Difficulty
5 = Independent
N/A = Not Applicable
Funding Source
Financial Classification
The funding category for an IHSS case determining how services are paid for. Major funding sources include SSI/SSP (federal/state), Residual (state-only), PCSP (Personal Care Services Program), and IPO (In-Person Operations).
G
Good Cause
Exception
A valid reason for an exception to standard rules or requirements. In IHSS, good cause may apply to deadline extensions (if applicant hospitalized), EVV exceptions (no cell service, natural disaster), or processing delays (circumstances beyond county control).
H
Health Care Certification
Form SOC 873
A form completed by the applicant's physician that certifies the medical need for IHSS services. The health care certification documents functional limitations and medical conditions requiring in-home assistance.
HM
Homemaker Mode of Service
Homemaker mode of service where services are provided through a specialized homemaker/personal attendant contract program. Homemaker mode requires special timesheet approval workflow and is only available in counties with such contracts.
I
ICR
Intelligent Character Recognition Technology
Technology used by TPF vendor to recognize handwritten text on paper timesheets. ICR is more advanced than OCR and can interpret handwriting, though with lower accuracy (60-80%) than printed text recognition.
ICT
Inter-County Transfer Case Transfer
The process of transferring an IHSS case from one county to another when a recipient moves. ICT ensures continuity of services and transfers all case history and authorizations to the receiving county.
IHSS
In-Home Supportive Services Program
A California program that pays for in-home care services for aged, blind, and disabled individuals who are unable to remain safely in their own homes without assistance. IHSS helps people live independently and avoid institutionalization.
Impairment Level
Classification
A determination of whether a recipient has a "Severe" or "Non-Severe" impairment. Impairment level is calculated based on functional rankings across service categories and affects the maximum hours that can be authorized.
Initial Assessment
Assessment Type
The first assessment conducted for a new IHSS applicant. The initial assessment establishes the baseline functional abilities and determines initial authorization of services.
IP
Individual Provider Mode of Service
The most common mode of service where the recipient acts as the employer and hires their own provider. The provider is paid directly by the state/county, and the recipient directs and supervises the provider's work.
IPO
In-Person Operations Funding Source
A funding source category in CMIPS for certain types of IHSS cases. IPO is used for specific program variations or pilot programs.
J
Journal Voucher
Financial Document
An accounting document that summarizes payments made to contract care agencies or homemaker vendors. Journal vouchers are used for county financial reconciliation and claiming.
L
Leave Status
Case Status
A temporary suspension of IHSS services when a recipient is hospitalized, in a nursing facility, out of state, or otherwise unable to receive in-home services. During leave, service authorization is suspended but the case remains open.
Legally Responsible Relative
Restriction
A person who has a legal obligation to care for the recipient. Legally responsible relatives generally cannot be paid IHSS providers. Examples include a spouse or parent of a minor child (unless exception granted).
Live-In Provider
Provider Type
A provider who resides in the same home as the recipient. Live-in status affects overtime calculations under FLSA and may allow different overtime thresholds depending on state law.
M
MEDS
Medi-Cal Eligibility Data System State System
The statewide system that stores Medi-Cal eligibility information. CMIPS interfaces with MEDS to verify recipient eligibility, retrieve aid codes, check Share of Cost, and validate point-of-service eligibility for timesheets.
MEDS POS
MEDS Point of Service Validation
Real-time verification of Medi-Cal eligibility for specific service dates when processing timesheets. MEDS POS ensures the recipient had active Medi-Cal coverage on each day services were provided.
Medi-Cal
Program
California's Medicaid program providing health coverage to low-income individuals. Active Medi-Cal eligibility is a prerequisite for receiving IHSS services (with limited exceptions for Residual program).
Mode of Service
Service Delivery
The method by which IHSS services are delivered to a recipient. The three modes are Individual Provider (IP), Contract Care (CC), and Homemaker (HM). Mode of service determines payment workflow and supervision requirements.
My Workspace
CMIPS Screen
The personalized dashboard in CMIPS showing a caseworker's assigned tasks, pending cases, due dates, and workload. My Workspace is the starting point for daily work and task management.
N
NOA
Notice of Action Legal Notice
A written notice sent to a recipient informing them of any action that affects their IHSS services (approval, denial, increase, reduction, termination). NOAs are required by law and must include: the action taken, effective date, reason, legal authority, and appeal rights.
NOA Code
Message Code
A two-letter code that identifies the specific situation and generates corresponding NOA text. Examples: AA (Application Approved), DN (Application Denied), HR (Hours Reduced), TR (Case Terminated). There are 27+ NOA codes in CMIPS.
O
OCR
Optical Character Recognition Technology
Technology used by TPF vendor to recognize printed text on paper timesheets. OCR has high accuracy (95%+) for pre-printed fields like names, dates, and timesheet numbers.
Overpayment
Financial
Payment made to a provider that exceeds the authorized amount or was made in error. Overpayments must be recovered from the provider through repayment plans or deductions from future payments.
Overtime
FLSA Requirement
Payment at 1.5 times the regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a provider's defined workweek. Overtime is required by FLSA to compensate providers fairly for extended work hours.
Overtime Violation
Compliance Issue
A violation that occurs when a provider works more than 40 hours per week without a proper workweek agreement or overtime authorization. Violations are tracked and can result in warnings or restrictions on the case.
P
PA
Public Authority Organization
A county-established entity that serves as the employer of record for IHSS providers. Public Authorities operate provider registries, negotiate wages and benefits, and provide training and support services.
Paramedical Services
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Medical support tasks including respiratory care, medication assistance (prompting and setup), wound care, blood pressure monitoring, and other health-related assistance that does not require a licensed medical professional.
PCSP
Personal Care Services Program Program/Funding
A specific IHSS program variant with its own funding source. PCSP serves certain recipient populations with particular eligibility criteria.
Person Home
CMIPS Screen
The central screen in CMIPS for an individual person (recipient, applicant, referral, or provider), providing access to demographic information, contact details, cases, and person-level notes.
Person Notes
Documentation
Chronological record of all contacts, conversations, and significant events related to a person. Person notes provide a complete history and are critical for case documentation, continuity of care, and audit compliance.
Personal Care Services
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Assistance with activities of daily living including bathing, dressing, grooming, eating, mobility, positioning, and transfers. Personal care is often the largest component of authorized hours.
Provider
Role
A person who provides IHSS services to a recipient. Providers must be at least 18 years old, pass a criminal background check (CORI), complete enrollment, and cannot be legally responsible relatives (with exceptions).
Provider Enrollment
Onboarding
The process of registering a new provider into the IHSS program. Enrollment includes completing forms (W-4, DE-4, I-9), submitting to CORI background check, and obtaining supervisor approval before the provider can be assigned to cases.
Protective Supervision
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Continuous supervision for recipients with severe mental impairments who are at substantial risk of harm to themselves or others. Protective supervision requires detailed documentation and cannot be self-directed.
Q
Quality Assurance (QA)
Oversight
Systematic monitoring and review of IHSS cases to ensure quality, accuracy, and compliance with regulations. QA activities include case reviews, assessment audits, random sampling, and unannounced home visits.
R
Reassessment
Assessment Type
An annual assessment conducted to verify continued eligibility and adjust authorized hours based on current functional abilities. Reassessments must be completed before the authorization period expires to maintain continuous services.
Recipient
Role
An individual who receives IHSS services. Recipients must be aged, blind, or disabled, have Medi-Cal eligibility, meet residency requirements, and be assessed as needing in-home assistance to safely remain in their own homes.
Referral
Initial Contact
An initial inquiry or contact about IHSS services. A referral captures basic information about someone who may be interested in applying but has not yet submitted a formal application.
Rescind
Action
To reverse or undo a case termination. A termination may be rescinded due to county error, successful state hearing appeal, or restoration of Medi-Cal eligibility. Rescission reinstates services and may trigger retroactive payments.
Residual
Funding Source
State-only IHSS funding for recipients who do not have federal Medi-Cal but meet IHSS eligibility requirements. Residual cases are funded entirely by California without federal financial participation.
S
SCI
Statewide Client Index State System
California's master person index for public assistance programs. SCI assigns a unique CIN to each person and prevents duplicate records across counties and programs.
Service Types
Classification
The eight categories of IHSS services: (1) Domestic Services, (2) Personal Care Services, (3) Bowel and Bladder Care, (4) Paramedical Services, (5) Accompanying to Medical Appointments, (6) Teaching and Demonstration, (7) Protective Supervision, (8) Disaster Preparedness.
Share of Cost (SOC)
Cost Sharing
A monthly amount that certain Medi-Cal recipients must pay toward their medical care, similar to an insurance deductible. Recipients with SOC have a portion of their IHSS hours designated as unpaid SOC hours that satisfy their monthly obligation.
SOC 873
Form
The IHSS application form and health care certification. SOC 873 is completed by the applicant and their physician to initiate the IHSS application process.
SOC 2261
Form
The standard IHSS timesheet form used by providers to report hours worked. SOC 2261 must be signed by both provider and recipient (unless signature waived) and submitted semi-monthly.
SOC 2255
Form
The Provider Workweek Agreement form. SOC 2255 establishes the provider's 7-consecutive-day workweek definition, which is necessary for calculating overtime correctly.
SOC 858
Form
The Provider Notification form automatically generated when recipient hours change, case is terminated, or other actions affect provider assignments. SOC 858 informs providers of changes to authorized hours.
Spend-Down
SOC Processing
The calculation process that determines how many provider hours are used to satisfy a recipient's Share of Cost obligation. Spend-down divides the month into workweeks and applies regular and overtime hours against the SOC amount until the SOC is met.
SSI/SSP
Supplemental Security Income / State Supplementary Payment Funding Source
A combined federal and state program providing monthly cash benefits to aged, blind, and disabled individuals. Recipients of SSI/SSP automatically qualify for Medi-Cal and represent the largest funding source for IHSS cases.
State Fair Hearing
Appeal
A formal appeal process where a recipient can challenge an adverse county decision. State hearings are conducted by the California Department of Social Services and provide due process rights. Recipients have 90 days to file an appeal.
Submit for Approval
CMIPS Function
The CMIPS function that formally submits a completed assessment for final approval. Depending on county policy, this either creates a task for supervisor approval or immediately processes the authorization.
T
Teaching and Demonstration
Service Category
One of the eight IHSS service categories. Training and instruction to help recipients learn to perform tasks they cannot currently do independently. Teaching hours are temporary and limited in duration.
Termination
Case Action
The permanent closure of an IHSS case when a recipient is no longer eligible for services. Common termination reasons include death, moving out of state, nursing home placement, voluntary withdrawal, or loss of Medi-Cal eligibility.
Tier 1 Conviction
CORI Result
Serious criminal convictions that permanently disqualify a person from being an IHSS provider with no possibility of waiver. Tier 1 convictions include murder, rape, child abuse, elder abuse, and other violent crimes against vulnerable adults.
Tier 2 Conviction
CORI Result
Criminal convictions that disqualify a person from being an IHSS provider unless the recipient signs a waiver acknowledging the conviction and choosing to hire the provider anyway. Tier 2 includes theft, fraud, drug offenses, and other less serious crimes.
Timesheet
Form
A form (SOC 2261 or electronic equivalent) that providers submit semi-monthly to report hours worked for a recipient. Timesheets must be signed by provider and recipient, validated against authorization, and approved before payment.
Timesheet Exception
Validation
A timesheet that fails validation and requires county review before payment. Common exceptions include: hours over authorization, MEDS POS errors, missing signatures, case terminated during pay period, or EVV discrepancies.
TPF
Third Party Forms Vendor
A vendor that processes paper IHSS timesheets through mailroom operations, high-speed scanning, OCR/ICR recognition, and data entry. TPF handles millions of paper timesheets annually and transmits data to CMIPS.
Travel Claim
Form
A form that providers submit to claim compensation for travel time between multiple recipients' homes. Travel time is only compensable when traveling directly from one recipient to another on the same workday.
Travel Time
Compensable Time
Time spent by a provider traveling directly from one recipient's home to another recipient's home on the same workday. Travel time must be authorized and claimed on a separate travel claim form.
TTS
Telephone Timesheet System IVR System
An Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system that allows providers to submit timesheets by telephone. Providers call a toll-free number (1-866-376-7066), authenticate with their Provider ID and PIN, and enter hours via phone keypad.
U
Unannounced Home Visit
Quality Assurance
A quality assurance visit conducted by county staff without advance notice to verify that services are being provided as authorized. Unannounced visits help detect fraud and ensure service quality.
V
Validation
Process
The automated checking of data against business rules to ensure accuracy, completeness, and compliance. Validation occurs throughout CMIPS for assessments, authorizations, timesheets, and payments.
W
Waiver
Exception
A signed document allowing an exception to a standard rule. In IHSS, recipients can sign waivers to: (1) hire providers with Tier 2 convictions, (2) hire legally responsible relatives in certain circumstances, or (3) waive recipient signature on timesheets if physically unable to sign.
Warrant
Payment
A payment check issued by the State Controller's Office. Warrants are issued to IHSS providers, contract agencies, and vendors. Tracking includes warrant issuance, stale-dating, redeposits, and voids.
Workweek
FLSA Requirement
A fixed, recurring period of seven consecutive 24-hour periods (168 hours) established for each provider to calculate overtime. The workweek must be consistent (e.g., Sunday through Saturday) and apply to all recipients a provider works for.
Workweek Agreement
Form SOC 2255
A signed agreement (SOC 2255) between provider and county establishing the provider's workweek definition. Without a workweek agreement, CMIPS cannot calculate overtime correctly.
WPCS
Waiver Personal Care Services Program
A Medicaid waiver program variant of IHSS. WPCS has special federal reporting requirements and different funding sources than standard IHSS.
