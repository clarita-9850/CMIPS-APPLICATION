Overview

Case Maintenance encompasses all ongoing operations performed by caseworkers to manage active recipient cases, including assessments, status changes, and documentation.

Reassessment & Renewals

â–¼

Periodic reassessments ensure continued eligibility and appropriate service levels.

Key Features:

  • Initial Assessment: Conducted at case approval
  • Reassessment: Scheduled review (typically annually)
  • Change Assessment: Triggered by changes in recipient circumstances
  • Renewal Process: Case authorization renewal before expiration

Assessment Types

  • Initial: First assessment for new applicants
  • Reassessment: Scheduled annual or periodic review
  • Change: Assessment due to reported changes
  • Special: Unscheduled assessment for specific reasons

Medi-Cal Share of Cost Processing

â–¼

Manage recipients with Medi-Cal Share of Cost (SOC) obligations through Point of Service Spend-down processing.

What is Share of Cost?

Share of Cost is the amount a recipient must pay toward their medical expenses each month before Medi-Cal coverage begins, similar to an insurance deductible.

Operations:

  • View Medi-Cal Eligibility: Review recipient's Medi-Cal status and SOC amount
  • Share of Cost Details: View monthly SOC amounts and spend-down status
  • Modify Eligibility: Update Medi-Cal information when changes occur
  • SAWS Integration: Automatic updates from county welfare system

Point of Service Spend-down

Recipients with SOC can "spend down" their obligation by incurring medical expenses (including IHSS services). Once SOC is met, Medi-Cal covers remaining expenses for that month.

Monthly Tracking:

System tracks monthly spend-down progress and generates appropriate letters to recipients and providers.

10 Recipient Flexible Hours

â–¼

Manage flexible hour arrangements for recipients with variable care needs.

Flexible Hours Management

â–¼

Purpose:

Flexible hours allow recipients to use their authorized hours more flexibly across weeks rather than strict weekly limits, accommodating variable care needs.

Operations:

  • Create Flexible Hours: Establish flexible hour arrangement
  • View Flexible Hours: Review current flexible hour setup
  • Modify Flexible Hours: Update flexible hour parameters
  • Flexible Hours History: Track changes over time
  • Inactivate Flexible Hours: End flexible hour arrangement

Flexible Hours Example

A recipient authorized for 80 hours/month (approximately 20 hours/week) might use flexible hours to work:

  • Week 1: 15 hours
  • Week 2: 25 hours
  • Week 3: 20 hours
  • Week 4: 20 hours

Total: 80 hours for the month, even though weekly hours vary.
