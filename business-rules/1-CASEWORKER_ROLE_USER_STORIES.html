IHSS Caseworker Role Overview

As an IHSS caseworker, I am responsible for managing recipient cases from initial application through ongoing services. My primary responsibilities include:

  • Intake & Application Processing: Receiving and processing new IHSS applications within 45-day federal timeline
  • Needs Assessment: Conducting comprehensive in-home assessments to evaluate recipient functional abilities
  • Eligibility Determination: Making final eligibility decisions and determining authorized service hours
  • Provider Management: Enrolling, assigning, and managing IHSS providers for my cases
  • Ongoing Case Maintenance: Conducting annual reassessments, processing changes, maintaining case records
  • Timesheet & Payment Oversight: Reviewing and approving provider timesheets for payment
  • Compliance & Documentation: Ensuring all actions comply with regulations and are properly documented

1 Intake & Application Processing

‚ñº

Source: DSD Section 20 - Online Search, Initial Contact, Intake Application

US-001: Search for Existing Person Records

As a caseworker, I search for existing person records in CMIPS before creating new ones, so that I can avoid creating duplicate records and maintain data integrity.
I can:
  • Search by name, SSN, date of birth, CIN, or person ID
  • Use wildcard searches to find similar names
  • Filter results by county and person type
  • View person details before deciding to create new record
  • Access Person Home screen directly from search results

US-002: Create Referral for Initial Contact

As a caseworker, I create referrals when someone contacts the county inquiring about IHSS, so that I can track initial contacts and follow up appropriately.
I can:
  • Capture caller's basic information (name, DOB, contact info)
  • Record referral source (self, family, hospital, medical provider, agency)
  • Document referral reason and initial conversation
  • Assign referral to myself or another worker
  • Schedule follow-up contact

US-003: Create and Process Applications

As a caseworker, I create formal IHSS applications when individuals decide to apply for services, so that I can begin the eligibility determination process within the required 45-day timeline.
I can:
  • Create application with application date (starts 45-day clock)
  • Enter personal, contact, eligibility, medical, and financial information
  • Perform CIN clearance with Statewide Client Index
  • Verify Medi-Cal eligibility through MEDS interface
  • Track application processing days remaining
  • Document any delays or extensions with good cause

üìãDaily Task: Process New Applications

My typical workflow:
  1. Review new applications assigned to my queue
  2. Contact applicant to schedule in-home assessment
  3. Verify all required documentation is received (application form SOC 873, medical records, proof of residency)
  4. Check Medi-Cal eligibility status in MEDS
  5. Perform CIN clearance if not already done
  6. Document all contacts and actions in Person Notes
  7. Schedule assessment within timeframe to meet 45-day deadline

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Deadline

Federal law requires I process applications within 45 days from application date. I must closely monitor my pending applications and take action before deadlines expire.

2 Conducting Needs Assessments

‚ñº

Source: DSD Section 21 - Service Eligibility (Assessments, Evidence, Health Care Certification)

US-004: Conduct Comprehensive In-Home Assessment

As a caseworker, I conduct in-home assessments to evaluate the applicant's functional abilities and determine service needs, so that I can make accurate eligibility and authorization decisions.
I can:
  • Create assessment record (Initial, Reassessment, or Change Assessment)
  • Schedule and document in-home visit
  • Evaluate functional abilities across 8 service categories
  • Rank functional index levels (1-5 or N/A) for each task
  • Document medical conditions and how they impact functioning
  • Assess living environment and safety concerns
  • Write comprehensive assessment narrative

Assessment Workflow I Follow

Step 1: Pre-Assessment Preparation

I review medical documentation, previous assessments (if any), and application information before the home visit.

Step 2: In-Home Visit

I visit the applicant's home, observe their environment, and evaluate their ability to perform daily living tasks. I interview the applicant and may speak with family or caregivers.

Step 3: Functional Ranking

I rank each task using the functional index: 1 (Unable/Unable to Participate), 2 (Needs Assistance), 3 (Needs Supervision), 4 (Independent with Difficulty), 5 (Independent), N/A (Not Applicable).

Step 4: Document Assessment

I enter all assessment evidence into CMIPS and write a detailed narrative explaining my observations and rankings.

Step 5: Review & Validate

I use "Check Eligibility" to preview the determination before submitting for approval.

US-005: Enter Assessment Evidence

As a caseworker, I enter detailed assessment evidence into CMIPS for each service category, so that the system can accurately calculate authorized hours and I can justify my determinations.
For each of 8 service categories, I can:
  • Domestic Services: Meal preparation, cleanup, laundry, shopping
  • Personal Care: Bathing, dressing, grooming, eating, mobility
  • Bowel/Bladder Care: Toileting, catheter care, ostomy care
  • Paramedical Services: Respiration, medication management, wound care
  • Accompanying to Medical: Transportation assistance to appointments
  • Teaching/Demonstration: Training on tasks they can't currently do
  • Protective Supervision: Continuous supervision for mental conditions
  • Disaster Preparedness: Planning for emergencies

US-006: Obtain Health Care Certification

As a caseworker, I obtain health care certification (SOC 873 form) from the applicant's physician, so that I can verify the medical need for IHSS services as required by regulations.
I can:
  • Send SOC 873 Health Care Certification form to physician
  • Track status: Sent, Received, Follow-up Needed
  • Review physician's attestation of functional limitations
  • Ensure certification received within required timeframe
  • Document form receipt date in CMIPS
  • Follow up if form not returned within 10 days

üè†Daily Task: Complete Assessment Narratives

I write narratives that include:
  1. Medical Background: Diagnosis, conditions, how they affect functioning
  2. Observations: What I observed during home visit
  3. Functional Limitations: Specific tasks applicant cannot perform independently
  4. Living Environment: Home safety, accessibility, available assistance
  5. Service Justification: Why each authorized service is medically necessary
  6. Protective Supervision Justification: If applicable, detailed explanation of mental impairment and continuous supervision need

3 Making Eligibility Determinations

‚ñº

Source: DSD Section 22 - Final Determination, Assign Modes of Service

US-007: Check Eligibility Before Submission

As a caseworker, I use "Check Eligibility" to preview the determination outcome, so that I can identify and fix any errors before officially submitting the case for approval.
I can:
  • Click "Check Eligibility" after entering all assessment evidence
  • Review preliminary eligibility outcome (approve, deny, hours)
  • Identify any validation errors or warnings
  • Review which business rules triggered
  • See preliminary NOA codes that would be generated
  • Make corrections and re-check before final submission
  • Confirm this does NOT generate Notice of Action

US-008: Submit Case for Approval

As a caseworker, I submit completed assessments for approval (either supervisor approval or self-approval based on county policy), so that cases can be authorized and recipients can begin receiving services.
I can:
  • Click "Submit for Approval" when assessment is complete
  • If supervisor approval required: create task for supervisor
  • If self-approval: immediately process business rules
  • Cancel my own submission before supervisor reviews (if needed)
  • View which supervisor the case is assigned to
  • Track submission status and approval timeline

US-009: Review Authorization Summary

As a caseworker, I review the Authorization Summary after approval, so that I can verify the correct hours were authorized and understand which NOA messages will be sent to the recipient.
I can see:
  • Authorization start and end dates (12-month period)
  • Funding source (SSI/SSP, Residual, PCSP, IPO)
  • Impairment level (Severe or Non-Severe)
  • Total authorized hours per month
  • Hours breakdown by service type
  • Mode of service (IP, CC, or HM)
  • All NOA codes that will print on recipient's notice
  • Share of Cost amounts (if applicable)

US-010: Assign Modes of Service

As a caseworker, I assign modes of service (Individual Provider, Contract Care, or Homemaker) to distribute authorized hours, so that recipients receive services in the most appropriate manner.
I can:
  • View that all hours default to Individual Provider (IP) mode initially
  • For mixed mode cases (IP & CC or IP & HM), manually distribute hours
  • Ensure total distributed hours don't exceed authorized hours
  • Document reason for Contract Care or Homemaker mode assignment
  • Modify mode distribution if recipient circumstances change
  • View mode of service history

My Final Determination Workflow

Complete Assessment Evidence Entry

I ensure all functional rankings, medical information, and narrative are complete.

Check Eligibility (Preview)

I click "Check Eligibility" to preview outcome and catch any errors.

Fix Any Errors

I correct any validation errors or missing information.

Submit for Approval

I formally submit the case, which sends it to supervisor (if required) or immediately processes.

Supervisor Approval (if required)

My supervisor reviews and approves or rejects. If rejected, I receive it back as a task with feedback.

Authorization Created

Upon approval, authorization is established and Notice of Action generated.

Assign Modes of Service

If mixed mode, I distribute hours between IP, CC, and/or HM.

‚úÖ What Happens After Approval

Once I approve a case:

  • Authorization period is established (typically 12 months)
  • Notice of Action (NOA) is automatically generated and mailed to recipient
  • Case status changes to "Active"
  • I can now assign providers to the case
  • Providers can begin submitting timesheets
  • Recipient can enroll in Electronic Services Portal (ESP)

4 Provider Management

‚ñº

Source: DSD Section 23 - Provider Management (Enrollment, CORI, Workweek, Overtime, Benefits)

US-011: Enroll New Providers

As a caseworker, I enroll new providers into the IHSS program, so that they can provide services to recipients and receive payment for their work.
I can:
  • Search for existing person records before creating new provider
  • Create new provider person record if not found
  • Enter required information: name, SSN, DOB, address, phone
  • Have provider complete enrollment forms (W-4, DE-4, I-9)
  • Set up direct deposit information
  • Verify provider is 18+ years old
  • Verify provider is not legally responsible relative (or exception applies)
  • Initiate CORI background check
  • Submit provider enrollment for supervisor approval

US-012: Process CORI Background Checks

As a caseworker, I initiate and review Criminal Offender Record Information (CORI) background checks for all providers, so that only qualified individuals with acceptable backgrounds can provide IHSS services.
I can:
  • Submit CORI request after provider completes enrollment
  • Track CORI status: Pending, Clear, Disqualifying Conviction Found
  • Review CORI results when returned
  • Identify Tier 1 convictions (permanently disqualifying, no waiver)
  • Identify Tier 2 convictions (disqualifying unless waiver granted)
  • Approve provider enrollment if CORI is clear
  • Deny provider enrollment if Tier 1 conviction found
  • Process recipient waiver request if Tier 2 conviction found

US-013: Assign Providers to Cases

As a caseworker, I assign approved providers to recipient cases with specific hours, so that recipients have designated caregivers who can submit timesheets and receive payment.
I can:
  • Assign only approved/active providers to cases
  • Specify assignment start date and hours
  • Assign regular IHSS hours, backup hours, WPCS hours
  • Assign travel time hours (if provider travels between recipients)
  • Ensure total assigned provider hours ‚â§ case authorized hours
  • Assign multiple providers to one case
  • View provider assignment history
  • Modify provider hours as needs change
  • Terminate provider assignment when needed

US-014: Create Provider Workweek Agreements

As a caseworker, I create Provider Workweek Agreements (SOC 2255), so that the system can correctly calculate overtime for providers who work more than 40 hours per week.
I can:
  • Create workweek agreement defining 7-consecutive-day workweek
  • Select workweek period (e.g., Sunday-Saturday, Monday-Sunday)
  • Ensure same workweek applies to all recipients provider works for
  • Obtain signed SOC 2255 form from provider
  • Document workweek agreement effective date
  • View workweek agreement history
  • Understand that without proper workweek, system cannot calculate overtime

US-015: Review and Resolve Overtime Violations

As a caseworker, I review overtime violations detected by CMIPS, so that I can determine if violations are valid and take appropriate action to ensure FLSA compliance.
I can:
  • View overtime violation when provider works >40 hrs/week without agreement
  • Review violation details (provider, recipient, hours, week)
  • Determine if violation is sustained or not sustained
  • If not sustained: document reason and dismiss violation
  • If sustained: issue warning (first offense) or penalty
  • Create Recipient Overtime Agreement to authorize future overtime
  • Request overtime exemption for extraordinary circumstances
  • Submit county review for supervisor approval
  • Track violations through 4-level review process (County‚ÜíState‚ÜíCDSS)

üë•Daily Task: Provider Maintenance

My regular provider management tasks:
  1. Review new provider enrollments awaiting approval
  2. Check CORI background check results
  3. Process provider assignment requests from recipients
  4. Update provider information (address changes, bank account updates)
  5. Create or update workweek agreements as needed
  6. Review overtime violation notifications and determine action
  7. Process provider termination requests
  8. Respond to provider inquiries via phone or Message Center

5 Timesheet & Payment Processing

‚ñº

Source: DSD Section 24 - Time & Attendance (Timesheets, Travel Claims, EVV, Payroll)

US-016: Review Timesheet Exceptions

As a caseworker, I review timesheets flagged with validation errors or exceptions, so that I can determine if timesheets should be approved, modified, or rejected before payment processing.
I can:
  • View list of timesheets in exception status assigned to me
  • See exception reason (hours over authorization, MEDS POS error, missing signature, etc.)
  • View timesheet image (for paper timesheets)
  • Review timesheet details and hours entered
  • Compare timesheet hours to authorized hours
  • Check recipient's eligibility status for service dates
  • Make decision: Approve, Modify Hours, Reject, or Cancel timesheet
  • Document reason for my decision

US-017: Review EVV Discrepancies

As a caseworker, I review timesheets where Electronic Visit Verification (EVV) data doesn't match the submitted timesheet hours, so that I can verify the accuracy of claimed hours and ensure proper payment.
I can:
  • View EVV check-in/check-out times for each service date
  • Compare EVV hours to timesheet claimed hours
  • See GPS location data (if using mobile EVV)
  • Review EVV exception reason (missing data, hours don't match, wrong location)
  • Determine if good cause exception applies (no cell service, natural disaster, technology failure, safety concerns)
  • Accept timesheet hours despite EVV mismatch (with documented justification)
  • Adjust timesheet hours to match EVV data
  • Reject timesheet and request provider resubmit

US-018: Approve Homemaker Timesheets

As a caseworker (for Homemaker mode cases), I review and approve timesheets before they go to payroll, so that I can verify services were provided as authorized.
I can:
  • View timesheets pending my approval (Homemaker/PA mode only)
  • Review timesheet details and hours
  • Verify hours are within authorized amounts
  • Approve timesheet to release for payroll
  • Reject timesheet with reason if issues found
  • Submit approved timesheets to supervisor (if dual approval required)

US-019: Process Travel Claims

As a caseworker, I review travel claim forms submitted by providers who travel between multiple recipients, so that I can approve compensable travel time in accordance with policy.
I can:
  • View travel claim form (paper or electronic)
  • Verify provider has authorized travel time between specific recipients
  • Confirm travel occurred between two recipient homes (not home to recipient or recipient to home)
  • Verify travel hours are within authorized limits
  • Ensure travel claim dates align with work dates on timesheets
  • Approve travel claim for payment
  • Reject travel claim if not eligible or exceeds authorization

US-020: Issue Replacement Timesheets

As a caseworker, I issue replacement timesheets when providers report lost or damaged forms, so that providers can continue submitting hours for payment without delay.
I can:
  • Search for recipient case or provider
  • Select pay period for which replacement is needed
  • Issue replacement timesheet (standard or large font)
  • Choose delivery method (mail to provider, mail to recipient, pickup at office)
  • Document reason for replacement issuance
  • Track that replacement was issued

‚è∞Daily Task: Timesheet Review Queue

Each day I work through my timesheet queue:
  1. Log in and review my timesheet exception task list
  2. Prioritize timesheets by pay period urgency (older pay periods first)
  3. Review each exception, determine root cause
  4. For MEDS POS errors: verify if recipient had Medi-Cal eligibility, may need to hold timesheet
  5. For over-authorization: check if hours legitimately needed or should be reduced
  6. For missing signatures: contact provider/recipient to obtain signature or approve electronically if policy allows
  7. For EVV discrepancies: review EVV data, determine good cause, make decision
  8. Document decision and reason in notes
  9. Approve, modify, or reject timesheets based on findings
  10. Respond to provider inquiries about rejected timesheets

‚ö†Ô∏è Timesheet Review Timeline

I must review and process timesheets promptly to ensure providers receive payment on time. Typical timeline:

  • Day 1-7: Provider submits timesheet (within 7 days after pay period ends)
  • Day 8-12: County receives and processes timesheet
  • Day 13-15: I review and approve exceptions
  • Day 16-18: Submit to payroll
  • Day 19-21: Payroll processing
  • Day 22+: Provider receives payment

6 Ongoing Case Maintenance

‚ñº

Source: DSD Section 25 - Case Maintenance (Reassessments, Changes, Leave, Termination)

US-021: Conduct Annual Reassessments

As a caseworker, I conduct annual reassessments for all active cases, so that I can verify continued eligibility and adjust authorized hours based on current needs.
I can:
  • View list of cases due for reassessment
  • Schedule reassessment within required timeframe (before authorization expires)
  • Create reassessment record in CMIPS
  • Conduct in-home visit and re-evaluate functional abilities
  • Enter new assessment evidence and compare to previous assessment
  • Determine if hours should increase, decrease, or stay the same
  • Submit reassessment for approval
  • Generate NOA explaining any changes in authorization

US-022: Process Change Assessments

As a caseworker, I conduct change assessments when recipient's condition or circumstances change mid-authorization, so that I can adjust services to meet current needs without waiting for annual reassessment.
I can:
  • Create change assessment when notified of recipient condition change
  • Document reason for change (medical condition worsened, improved, new diagnosis, hospitalization)
  • Conduct in-home visit to evaluate current functional status
  • Adjust authorized hours and services as needed
  • Create new authorization segment for changed hours
  • Generate NOA explaining the change
  • Ensure change assessment effective date is appropriate

US-023: Place Cases on Leave

As a caseworker, I place cases on temporary leave when recipients are hospitalized or in nursing facilities, so that IHSS services and payments are suspended during the time recipient is not receiving in-home services.
I can:
  • Document leave reason (hospitalization, nursing facility, out of state, incarceration)
  • Enter leave start date
  • Suspend service authorization during leave period
  • Generate NOA informing recipient of leave status
  • Track leave duration
  • Return recipient from leave when they return home
  • Resume services effective date of return

US-024: Terminate Cases

As a caseworker, I terminate cases when recipients are no longer eligible for IHSS, so that services and payments cease and case records reflect current status.
I can:
  • Document termination reason (no longer eligible, deceased, moved out of state, requested termination, Medi-Cal ended, nursing home placement)
  • Enter termination effective date
  • Generate 10-day advance NOA (except for death or voluntary withdrawal)
  • End all provider assignments effective termination date
  • Ensure final timesheets are processed
  • Close case in system
  • Process overpayments if services provided after termination date

US-025: Rescind Terminations

As a caseworker, I rescind case terminations when termination was done in error or when recipient successfully appeals, so that services can resume without requiring new application.
I can:
  • Document rescind reason (state hearing decision, county error, Medi-Cal restored)
  • Enter rescind effective date
  • Restore authorization to active status
  • Reactivate provider assignments
  • Generate NOA explaining reinstatement
  • Process retroactive payments if applicable

US-026: Maintain Case Documentation

As a caseworker, I maintain comprehensive case notes documenting all contacts, decisions, and significant events, so that case history is preserved and accessible for continuity of care, audits, and appeals.
I can:
  • Create case notes for every contact with recipient, provider, or family
  • Document phone calls, in-person visits, emails
  • Record decisions made and rationale
  • Note follow-up actions needed
  • Upload supporting documents (medical records, forms, correspondence)
  • View complete chronological case history
  • Search notes by date, type, or keyword

üìÖMonthly Task: Reassessment Planning

Each month I plan my reassessment schedule:
  1. Review list of cases with authorizations expiring in next 60-90 days
  2. Contact recipients to schedule reassessment home visits
  3. Prioritize cases expiring soonest
  4. Confirm recipient contact information is current
  5. Request updated medical records if needed
  6. Block calendar time for home visits
  7. Prepare by reviewing previous assessment and current services
  8. Complete reassessments at least 30 days before authorization expires to allow processing time

US-027: Handle State Fair Hearings

As a caseworker, I respond to state fair hearing requests filed by recipients appealing my decisions, so that I can provide documentation to support county's position and ensure recipients receive due process.
I can:
  • Receive notification when recipient files appeal
  • Review appeal reason and recipient's concerns
  • Gather supporting documentation (assessment, medical records, notes)
  • Prepare position statement explaining county decision
  • If aid paid pending: continue services during appeal
  • Attend state hearing (in person or by phone)
  • Implement hearing decision (uphold or overturn county action)
  • Process retroactive payments if hearing favorable to recipient

US-028: Process Inter-County Transfers (ICT)

As a caseworker, I process cases transferring to or from other counties when recipients move, so that services continue without interruption and all case information transfers properly.
When recipient moves to my county (ICT In), I can:
  • Receive case transfer from sending county
  • Review transferred case information and history
  • Verify recipient has moved into my county (proof of address)
  • Accept case transfer in CMIPS
  • Assign case to myself or another worker
  • Continue existing authorization or conduct new assessment if needed
  • Ensure providers are enrolled in my county
When recipient moves out of my county (ICT Out), I can:
  • Document recipient is moving to another county
  • Initiate case transfer to receiving county
  • Ensure all case documentation is current
  • Coordinate transfer date with receiving county
  • Close case in my county effective transfer date

US-029: Handle Protective Supervision Cases

As a caseworker, I carefully evaluate and document protective supervision cases, so that recipients with severe mental impairments receive appropriate continuous supervision services.
I can:
  • Identify potential protective supervision need during assessment
  • Obtain detailed mental health documentation
  • Evaluate if recipient meets protective supervision criteria (severe mental impairment + substantial risk of harm)
  • Document specific behaviors and risks in detail
  • Authorize protective supervision hours (separate from personal care)
  • Explain to family that protective supervision cannot be self-directed
  • Ensure protective supervision provider is not the recipient
  • Conduct more frequent reassessments for protective supervision cases

US-030: Respond to Recipient Inquiries and Complaints

As a caseworker, I respond promptly and professionally to recipient and provider inquiries and complaints, so that issues are resolved and customer service expectations are met.
I can:
  • Check my phone messages and Message Center daily
  • Return calls within 1 business day
  • Listen to concerns and gather information
  • Explain decisions and policies clearly
  • Resolve issues within my authority
  • Escalate to supervisor when appropriate
  • Document all contacts and resolutions
  • Follow up to ensure issue is resolved
  • Inform recipient of appeal rights when denying requests

üîçDaily Task: Task Queue Management

Each day I manage my task queue in My Workspace:
  1. Log in to CMIPS: Review My Workspace dashboard
  2. Sort by Priority: Urgent tasks (expiring authorizations, hearings) first
  3. Task Types I Handle:
    • New applications awaiting assessment
    • Assessments returned by supervisor (rejected, need corrections)
    • Timesheet exceptions requiring review
    • Overtime violations requiring determination
    • Provider enrollments awaiting approval
    • State hearing requests requiring response
    • Change requests from recipients
    • Cases due for reassessment
  4. Complete Tasks: Work through queue, highest priority first
  5. Document Actions: Add notes for each task completed
  6. Close Tasks: Mark tasks complete when resolved
