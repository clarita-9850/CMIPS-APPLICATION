About This Documentation

This document provides comprehensive specifications for all forms, notices, and communications used in the In-Home Supportive Services (IHSS) Case Management Information and Payrolling System (CMIPS). It serves as the single source of truth for all documentation requirements, including state-mandated forms (SOC), system-generated notices (NOA), electronic communications, and adaptive (fillable) forms. This documentation covers over 300 distinct forms and communications used across the IHSS program.

CMIPS One-Time Emails
â–¼

Purpose

Defines the business process for sending one-time email communications to providers and recipients about new training opportunities and system updates.

Process Overview

As of December 2022, CGI has updated this business process to notify all Providers and/or Recipients about new training opportunities via email communication system.

  • Triggered by system events or manual requests
  • Uses email templates defined in Section 1.1.26
  • Sent through CMIPS email service
  • Includes training announcements, system updates, and policy changes

Key Features

  • Targeted Distribution: Emails sent to specific user groups based on role (Provider/Recipient)

  • Audit Trail: All email communications are logged in CMIPS
  • Delivery Tracking: System tracks successful delivery and bounce-backs
CMIPS Forms Overview Process Flow
â–¼

Purpose

Provides a comprehensive overview of how forms are generated, processed, and distributed throughout the CMIPS system.

Form Generation Workflow

Step 1: Trigger Event

  • User action (case approval, provider assignment, etc.)
  • System event (batch processing, scheduled notifications)
  • External trigger (state mandate, policy change)

Step 2: Form Selection

  • System determines appropriate form based on event type
  • Checks recipient preferences (regular font vs. large font)
  • Verifies language requirements

Step 3: Data Population

  • Retrieves data from CMIPS database (recipients, cases, providers)
  • Populates form templates with dynamic data
  • Applies business rules and validation

Step 4: Generation & Distribution

  • Generates PDF, prints paper forms, or sends electronic communications
  • Routes to appropriate delivery channel (mail, email, ESP portal)
  • Creates audit log entry

Distribution Channels

Channel Form Types Recipients
U.S. Mail NOAs, SOC Forms, Letters All recipients and providers
Email Notifications, Confirmations ESP registered users
SMS Alerts, Verification codes Users with phone numbers

IHSS Notices of Action (NOA)
â–¼

Notices of Action are official state-mandated forms used to inform recipients of case decisions, changes, approvals, or denials. These are legal documents that trigger appeal rights.

Important Note

All NOA forms are available in both Regular Font and Large Font (L) versions to accommodate recipients with visual impairments. The system automatically selects the appropriate version based on recipient preferences.

NA 1250 â€“ IHSS Approval
â–¼

Form Purpose

Notifies recipients that their IHSS application has been approved and services will begin. This is the initial approval notice sent when a case is first approved.

Approval Notice Required Form

When Generated

  • Case status changes from PENDING to ELIGIBLE
  • Service eligibility assessment completed
  • Authorized hours and services determined
  • Health care certification (SOC 873) approved

Key Information Included

  • Recipient Information: Name, case number, CIN
  • Approval Date: Effective date of services
  • Authorized Services: List of approved service categories (Domestic, Related Services, Protective Supervision)
  • Authorized Hours: Monthly and weekly hour limits
  • Service Restrictions: Any limitations on services
  • Appeal Rights: Instructions for filing an appeal
  • Legal Authority: Citation of applicable regulations

Distribution

Mailed to recipient's address of record within 10 days of case approval.

Related Forms

  • NA 1251: Continuation pages if needed
  • GEN 1365: General continuation form
  • NA Back Page: Standard appeal rights information

Provides additional pages for NA 1250 when the approval notice requires more space to list all authorized services, hours, or special instructions.

Continuation Page

NA 1252 â€“ IHSS Denial NOA
â–¼

Form Purpose

Notifies applicants that their IHSS application has been denied and provides the reasons for denial along with appeal rights.

Denial Notice Required Form

When Generated

  • Case status set to DENIED
  • Applicant does not meet eligibility criteria
  • Income exceeds program limits
  • Medical necessity not established
  • Functional assessment indicates no need for services

Key Information Included

  • Denial Reasons: Specific explanation of why application was denied
  • Regulatory Basis: Citation of regulations supporting denial
  • Effective Date: Date denial takes effect
  • Appeal Rights: Detailed instructions on how to file an appeal (must file within 90 days)
  • State Hearing Information: How to request a state hearing
  • Aid Paid Pending: Information about continuing services during appeal

Common Denial Reasons

Reason Code Description
INCOME Income exceeds Medi-Cal eligibility limits
MEDICAL Does not meet medical necessity criteria
FUNCTIONAL Functional assessment indicates independence
RESIDENCE Not residing in qualifying residence type
AGE Does not meet age requirements
NA 1253 â€“ IHSS Change
â–¼

Form Purpose

Notifies recipients of changes to their authorized services, hours, or case status after initial approval. This is the most commonly used NOA.

Change Notice Required Form

When Generated

  • Change in authorized hours (increase or decrease)
  • Addition or removal of service categories
  • Change in service restrictions
  • Provider assignment changes
  • Reassessment results in service modifications
  • Policy changes affecting services

Key Information Included

  • What Changed: Clear description of the change
  • Reason for Change: Explanation of why change is occurring
  • Previous Authorization: What was authorized before
  • New Authorization: What is authorized now
  • Effective Date: When change takes effect
  • Impact on Services: How change affects recipient
  • Appeal Rights: Options for appealing the change

Types of Changes

Change Type Description Typical Trigger
Hours Increase More hours authorized Condition worsened, new services added
Hours Decrease Fewer hours authorized Condition improved, services no longer needed
Service Addition New service category added New need identified in reassessment
Service Removal Service category removed Service no longer medically necessary
Provider Change Provider assignment modified Provider termination, recipient request

NA 1255 â€“ IHSS Termination
â–¼

Form Purpose

Notifies recipients that their IHSS services are being terminated and provides the reason for termination along with appeal rights.

Termination Notice Required Form

When Generated

  • Case status set to TERMINATED
  • Recipient no longer meets eligibility criteria
  • Recipient moved out of state or to ineligible residence
  • Recipient deceased
  • Recipient voluntarily requests termination
  • Failure to comply with program requirements
  • Fraud or abuse determination

Key Information Included

  • Termination Reason: Clear explanation of why services are ending
  • Effective Date: Last date of service provision
  • Services Ending: List of all services being terminated
  • Final Authorization: Hours authorized through end date
  • Appeal Rights: Instructions for appealing termination
  • Aid Paid Pending: Options for continuing services during appeal
  • Reapplication Information: How to reapply if circumstances change

Common Termination Reasons

  • No Longer Medically Eligible: Condition improved, services no longer necessary
  • Moved to Ineligible Residence: Entered skilled nursing facility or other non-qualifying residence
  • Income Change: Income now exceeds program limits
  • Voluntary Termination: Recipient requests services end
  • Non-Compliance: Failure to complete required forms or assessments
  • Death: Recipient deceased
NA 1256 â€“ Share of Cost (IHSS-R)
â–¼

Form Purpose

Notifies recipients with Share of Cost (SOC) Medi-Cal that they must meet their monthly SOC before IHSS services can be paid. This applies to IHSS Residual (IHSS-R) cases.

Share of Cost Notice Required Form

When Generated

  • Case type is IHSS Residual (IHSS-R)
  • Recipient has Share of Cost Medi-Cal
  • SOC amount changes
  • SOC must be met before services paid

Key Information Included

  • Share of Cost Amount: Monthly amount recipient must pay
  • Explanation: How SOC works and why it applies
  • Payment Process: How to pay or meet SOC
  • Authorized Services: Services available after SOC is met
  • Provider Payment: How providers are paid when SOC applies
  • Tracking: How county tracks SOC payments

Share of Cost Process

Step 1: Recipient incurs medical expenses (including IHSS)

Step 2: Medical bills applied toward monthly SOC

Step 3: Once SOC met, Medi-Cal pays remaining IHSS costs

Step 4: Process resets each month

Overtime Violation Processing Forms
â–¼

Overtime violation forms manage Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) compliance. Providers who exceed workweek or travel time limits receive progressive notices and may face ineligibility after fourth violation.

FLSA Overtime Rules

Under FLSA, providers cannot work more than 40 hours per week for a single recipient without triggering overtime pay (time and a half). Travel time limited to 7 hours per week. The overtime violation system tracks and enforces these limits through progressive enforcement.

Violation Notice Forms (IHSS)
â–¼

SOC 2257 â€“ Notice to Provider of Overtime Violation

Official notice sent to provider when they exceed workweek or travel time limits.

  • Triggers:
    • Provider works more than 40 hours in workweek for single recipient
    • Provider claims more than 7 hours travel time in workweek
    • No valid exception or alternate schedule in place
  • Content:
    • Violation number (1st, 2nd, 3rd, or 4th)
    • Workweek dates and hours worked
    • Authorized hours vs. hours claimed
    • Explanation of violation
    • Consequences for current violation level
    • Instructions to avoid future violations
    • Right to dispute (after 1st and 2nd violations)
  • Progressive Consequences:
    • 1st Violation: Warning, educational materials provided
    • 2nd Violation: Required training completion
    • 3rd Violation: 30-day suspension from working for that recipient
    • 4th Violation: One-year statewide ineligibility

    • Consequences for provider
    • Impact on recipient's services
    • Options for recipient (keep provider with corrected hours, find new provider)
    • Recipient's responsibility to ensure compliance
  • Action Required: Recipient must work with provider to prevent future violations

Error Message System

CMIPS uses 260+ standardized error messages to guide users through form completion. Error messages are categorized by severity: Error (red), Warning (yellow), Info (blue), Success (green).

Error Message Categories

Error messages are numbered 1-260 and organized into categories:

  • 1-20: Required field errors
  • 21-40: Format validation errors
  • 41-60: Date and time validation
  • 61-80: Business rule violations
  • 81-100: Data integrity errors
  • 101-120: Relationship validation
  • 121-140: Authorization errors
  • 141-160: System validation
  • 161-180: Document upload errors
  • 181-200: Signature validation
  • 201-220: Conditional logic errors
  • 221-240: Data consistency checks
  • 241-260: System and network errors

Error Message Best Practices

  • Specific: Tells user exactly what's wrong and how to fix it
  • Timely: Appears immediately when error detected
  • Positioned: Displayed near the field with error
  • Color-Coded: Red for errors, yellow for warnings, blue for info, green for success

  • Accessible: Screen reader compatible, keyboard navigable
  • Actionable: Provides clear next steps

Form Submission Validation

Before form submission, system performs comprehensive validation:

  • Step 1: Check all required fields completed
  • Step 2: Validate all field formats and data types
  • Step 3: Verify business rules compliance
  • Step 4: Check data consistency across form
  • Step 5: Verify signatures present (if required)
  • Step 6: Validate attachments (if required)
  • Step 7: Check authorization and permissions
  • Result: If any validation fails, form not submitted, errors highlighted

Error Prevention Features

  • Input Masks: Format phone numbers, SSN, dates automatically as user types
  • Dropdowns: Use dropdowns instead of free text where possible to prevent errors
  • Auto-Complete: Suggest values based on partial input
  • Field Constraints: Limit input to valid characters (numbers only, letters only, etc.)
  • Visual Cues: Show required fields with asterisk (*)
  • Progressive Disclosure: Show fields only when relevant, reducing complexity
  • Help Text: Provide examples and instructions for each field
  • Tooltips: Hover/click for detailed field explanations
Form Submission & Processing
â–¼

Submission Workflow

Step 1: Form Completion

  • User fills out all required fields
  • Real-time validation throughout
  • Auto-save preserves progress

Step 2: Review & Validation

  • User clicks "Review" or "Submit"
  • System performs final validation
  • Summary page shows all entered information
  • User confirms accuracy

Step 3: Digital Signature (if required)

  • User signs electronically (mouse, touchscreen, or typed)
  • Signature time-stamped
  • IP address logged for audit trail

Step 4: Submission

  • Form data encrypted and transmitted to CMIPS
  • Submission receipt generated
  • Email confirmation sent
  • Form appears in user's ESP "Submitted Forms" section

Step 5: County Review

  • Form appears in county worker's queue
  • Worker reviews and processes
  • May approve, reject, or request additional information

Step 6: Outcome Notification

  • User notified via email and SMS (if opted in)
  • Status visible in ESP
  • If rejected, reason provided with option to correct and resubmit
