Overview

IMPORTANT

Providers CANNOT self-enroll or search for recipients themselves. The process is managed by county workers.

This guide walks you through the complete journey as an IHSS provider, from expressing initial interest to successfully serving recipients.

PART 1: Provider Enrollment (County-Initiated)

Step 1: Provider Expresses Interest

What You Do:

  • Contact your local county IHSS office (phone/walk-in)
  • Express interest in becoming an IHSS provider
  • Provide basic information (name, phone, email)

What Happens Behind the Scenes:

  • County worker gathers your information
  • County worker will create your provider record in CMIPS

Step 2: County Worker Creates Your Provider Record

This is NOT self-service - County worker does this:

  1. County Worker logs into CMIPS (county worker dashboard)
  2. County Worker navigates to: My Workspace ‚Üí Register a Provider
  3. County Worker searches for you (to see if you already exist in system)
  4. If not found: County Worker creates new provider record

System Actions:

  • Provider number generated (9-digit number, e.g., "123456789")
  • Initial status: eligible = 'PENDING'
  • SSN verification status: 'NOT_YET_VERIFIED'
  • Provider status: 'ACTIVE'

Where Stored:

  • PostgreSQL Database: providers table
  • Record created with your basic information

Step 3: You Complete Enrollment Requirements

County worker gives you forms and instructions:

3A. SOC 426 - Provider Enrollment Form

  • Fill out and return to county
  • County worker marks this complete in CMIPS

3B. Provider Orientation

  • Attend orientation session (county arranges)
  • County worker marks this complete in CMIPS

3C. DOJ Background Check

  • Submit fingerprints to Department of Justice
  • Wait for clearance
  • County worker marks this complete in CMIPS when cleared

3D. SOC 846 - Provider Enrollment Agreement

  • Sign provider agreement (required)
  • Sign overtime agreement (required after FLSA date)
  • Return to county
  • County worker marks this complete in CMIPS

3E. CORI Check (if applicable)

If background check finds convictions:

  • Tier 1: Cannot serve (automatically ineligible)
  • Tier 2: Can serve with waiver (county processes)

Where Stored:

Each requirement completion updates the providers table:

  • soc426_completed = true, soc426_date = '2025-01-16'
  • orientation_completed = true, orientation_date = '2025-01-17'
  • background_check_completed = true, background_check_date = '2025-01-20'
  • soc846_completed = true, provider_agreement_signed = true, etc.

Step 4: SSN Verification (Automated - Weekly Batch)

You don't do anything - System processes automatically:

  • Weekly batch job sends your SSN to Social Security Administration
  • SSA verifies your SSN
  • System updates your record:
    • ssn_verification_status = 'VERIFIED' (if valid)
    • OR: 'DECEASED', 'DUPLICATE_SSN', 'SUSPECT_SSN' (if issues found)

Where Stored:

providers table: ssn_verification_status field updated

Step 5: County Approves Your Enrollment

County worker clicks "Approve Enrollment" button:

System Validates:

  • ‚úÖ SOC 426 completed
  • ‚úÖ Orientation completed
  • ‚úÖ SOC 846 completed
  • ‚úÖ Background check completed
  • ‚úÖ Provider Agreement signed
  • ‚úÖ Overtime Agreement signed (if after FLSA)
  • ‚úÖ No active Tier 1 CORI
  • ‚úÖ Tier 2 CORI has waiver (if applicable)

If all requirements met:

  • Your status changes: eligible = 'PENDING' ‚Üí eligible = 'YES'
  • effective_date = Today's date
  • original_hire_date = Today's date

Where Stored:

providers table updated: eligible = 'YES'

üéâ You are now ELIGIBLE to serve!

PART 2: Getting Assigned to Recipients

IMPORTANT: You CANNOT search for or apply to recipients yourself. There are TWO WAYS you get assigned:

Pathway A: County Assigns You (Traditional)

How It Works:

  1. County worker assigns you to a recipient case
    • County worker selects a recipient who needs services
    • County worker searches for providers (including you)
    • County worker assigns you to the case
  2. You receive notification
    • Email or phone call from county
    • Information about recipient and case
  3. System creates provider assignment
    • Record created in provider_assignments table
    • Links you (provider_id) to recipient (recipient_id) and case (case_id)
    • Status: 'ACTIVE'
    • Begin date set

Where Stored:

provider_assignments table:

  • provider_id = Your Provider ID
  • recipient_id = Recipient ID
  • case_id = Case ID
  • status = 'ACTIVE'
  • begin_date = Today

Pathway B: Recipient Hires You (Self-Service - Recipient-Initiated)

How It Works:

  1. Recipient searches for providers (through ESP portal)
    • Recipient logs into ESP
    • Recipient goes to "Hire Provider"
    • Recipient searches by: Provider Name, Provider Number, or Last 4 of SSN
  2. Recipient finds and selects you
    • Your provider information appears in search results
    • Recipient reviews your eligibility status
    • Recipient selects "Hire Provider"
  3. Recipient completes hire process
    • Recipient confirms details
    • Recipient submits electronic signature
    • System creates provider assignment
  4. You receive notification
    • Email notification sent to you
    • Assignment information provided
    • County processes within 1-2 business days

Where Stored:

  • Same as Pathway A: provider_assignments table created
  • System also updates providers table: numberOfActiveCases incremented

You CANNOT initiate this process - only recipients can hire providers through ESP.

PART 3: ESP Registration (Self-Service - Optional but Recommended)

Once you're enrolled and eligible, you can register for ESP to submit timesheets electronically.

Step 1: Go to ESP Website

  • URL: https://www.etimesheets.ihss.ca.gov
  • Click "Register as New User"

Step 2: Complete 5-Step ESP Registration

Step 2.1: Validate Identity

You Enter:

  • Select "I am a Provider"
  • Provider Number: Your 9-digit provider number (from county)
  • Last Name, First Name, Date of Birth
  • Last 4 digits of SSN

System Validates:

  • All fields must match your CMIPS record exactly
  • You must be eligible (eligible = 'YES')
  • You cannot already be registered (unless inactive)

If validation fails:

  • Error: "Provider information does not match our records"
  • Contact county for help

Step 2.2: Verify Email

You Enter:

  • Email address (twice for confirmation)

System Actions:

  • Sends 6-digit verification code to your email
  • Code expires in 15 minutes

You Enter:

  • Verification code

System Validates:

  • Code must match and not be expired

Step 2.3: Create Username

You Enter:

  • Username (6-20 characters, letters/numbers/underscores/hyphens only)
  • Confirm username

System Validates:

  • Username must be unique
  • Real-time validation shows ‚úì or ‚úó

Step 2.4: Create Password

You Enter:

  • Password (must meet requirements):
    • Minimum 8 characters, maximum 50
    • At least one uppercase letter
    • At least one lowercase letter
    • At least one number
    • At least one special character (!@#$%^&*)
  • Confirm password

System Validates:

  • Password strength indicator (weak/medium/strong)
  • Cannot contain username
  • Cannot match last 5 passwords

Step 2.5: Security Questions

You Select & Answer:

  • 3 different security questions from dropdown
  • Answers (minimum 3 characters each)
  • Answers are case-insensitive
  • Cannot be username or password

Examples:

  • What is your mother's maiden name?
  • What city were you born in?
  • What is your favorite color?

Step 3: Registration Complete

Success!

  • Welcome email sent
  • Username reminder provided
  • You can now log in to ESP

System Updates:

providers table: esp_registered = true

PART 4: Using ESP as a Provider

After Logging In to ESP

You Can:

  1. View Your Recipients
    • See list of all recipients you're assigned to
    • View recipient names, case numbers, authorized hours
  2. Submit Timesheets
    • Enter time worked for each recipient
    • Daily or weekly entry format
    • Submit electronically
  3. View Payment History
    • See last 3 months of payments
    • Download pay stubs
    • View W-2 forms
  4. Submit Sick Leave Claims
    • Claim accrued sick leave hours
    • View available sick leave balance
  5. Manage Direct Deposit
    • Enroll in direct deposit
    • Update bank information
    • Cancel direct deposit
  6. Submit Travel Claims
    • Claim travel time (up to 7 hours/week)
    • Must have workweek agreement with travel
  7. Career Pathways
    • Submit training time
    • Claim incentives

You CANNOT:

  • ‚ùå Apply to recipients
  • ‚ùå See recipients who haven't hired you
  • ‚ùå Hire yourself to recipients

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I search for recipients who need providers?

A: No. Providers cannot search for or apply to recipients. Recipients search for and hire providers, or county workers assign providers to cases.

Q: How do I get assigned to a recipient?

A: Two ways:

  1. County assigns you - County worker selects you for a recipient case
  2. Recipient hires you - Recipient finds you through ESP and hires you

Q: Can I register for ESP before being assigned to a recipient?

A: Yes! You can register for ESP as soon as you're eligible (eligible = 'YES'). You don't need to wait for an assignment.

Q: What if I want to serve a specific recipient I know?

A: You can:

  1. Tell the recipient to search for you in ESP and hire you
  2. Contact your county worker to request assignment
  3. Have the recipient contact their case worker to request you

Q: How do I see my assigned recipients?

A: After logging into ESP, you'll see a list of all recipients you're assigned to. You can submit timesheets for each recipient.

Q: Can I see recipients who haven't hired me yet?

A: No. You can only see recipients you're already assigned to through provider assignments.
