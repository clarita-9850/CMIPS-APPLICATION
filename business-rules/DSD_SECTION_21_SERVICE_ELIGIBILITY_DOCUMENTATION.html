Document Title: DSD Section 21 - Recipient Case Management & Ongoing Services - Service Eligibility

Scope: Complete technical specifications for managing service eligibility determinations, assessments, and health care certifications

Audience: Caseworkers, assessors, supervisors, system developers, and business analysts

Purpose: Define all processes, screens, and business rules for determining recipient eligibility and authorized service hours

Key Forms: SOC 293 (Assessment Form), SOC 873 (Health Care Certification), SOC 874 (Alternative Documentation)

1 Service Eligibility Evidence Types

â–¼

Overview

Service eligibility is determined through the collection and evaluation of various types of evidence about the applicant/recipient's circumstances, needs, and functional abilities.

Household Evidence

â–¼

Information associated with the applicant/recipient's home environment and household members.

Key Components:

  • Residence Information: Living arrangement, residence type, home ownership status
  • Household Members: All people living in the residence
  • Companion Cases: Link multiple recipients in the same household
  • Household Composition: Family relationships and dependencies

Living Arrangements:

Type Description
Own Home Recipient owns or rents their own home/apartment
Lives with Others Recipient lives in someone else's home
Alternative Living Facility Licensed care facility (ARF, RCFE)
Independent Living Apartment or housing with minimal assistance

Household Member Operations:

  • Create Household Member: Add person to household
  • Modify Household Member: Update member information
  • View Household Member: Review member details
  • Relationship Tracking: Define relationships (spouse, parent, child, other)

Service Evidence

â–¼

Information specific to the applicant/recipient's functional ability within their home and their service needs.

Functional Rankings:

Recipients are assessed on their functional abilities across multiple areas:

Domestic Services

  • Meal Preparation
  • Meal Cleanup
  • Heavy Cleaning
  • Laundry
  • Shopping

Related Services

  • Yard Hazard Abatement
  • Accompaniment

Personal Care

  • Ambulation
  • Transfer
  • Repositioning
  • Bathing/Oral Hygiene
  • Bowel/Bladder Care
  • Dressing
  • Feeding/Eating
  • Respiration

Functional Index Levels:

Level Description Example
1 Independent Can perform task without assistance
2 Verbal Assistance Needs reminders or verbal cues
3 Verbal Assistance & Standby Needs someone present for safety
4 Some Physical Assistance Can do part of task, needs help with rest
5 Total Physical Assistance Cannot perform task, provider does everything

Service Type Details:

  • Task Frequency: How often task is performed (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • Time Allocation: Minutes needed per task occurrence
  • Assessed Need: System-calculated hours based on functional rankings
  • Authorized Hours: Final approved hours (may differ from assessed need)
  • Proration: Adjustment when multiple providers share tasks

Program Evidence

â–¼

Information specific to the IHSS Service Assessment period and program participation.

Program Evidence Components:

  • Assessment Begin Date: Start date for service eligibility period
  • Assessment End Date: End date for authorization (typically 12 months)
  • Assessed Need (Total Hours): System-calculated total based on all service tasks
  • Authorized Hours: County-approved hours (not to exceed assessed need)
  • Program Type: IHSS program variant (Regular, IHSS Plus, Residual, etc.)
  • County Pay Rate: Hourly wage for providers in this county

Manual Notice of Action (NOA)

Counties can generate manual NOAs when:

  • Authorized hours differ from assessed need
  • Special circumstances require explanation
  • Additional clarification needed for recipient

SAWS Notification:

Program evidence changes trigger automatic notifications to the Statewide Automated Welfare System (SAWS) to inform the county welfare department of IHSS case status.

Share of Cost (SOC) Evidence

â–¼

Information about the net income of the applicant/recipient to determine whether they have an associated Share of Cost obligation.

What is Share of Cost?

Recipients with income above Medi-Cal limits may still qualify for IHSS but must pay a monthly Share of Cost (like an insurance deductible) before Medi-Cal coverage begins.

SOC Evidence Operations:

  • Create SOC Evidence: Add income and expense information
  • Modify SOC Evidence: Update financial data
  • Delete SOC Evidence: Remove incorrect entries
  • Income Evidence: Track all income sources
  • SOC Hours Details: Calculate hours that count toward spend-down

Income Sources:

Source Type Description
SSI/SSP Supplemental Security Income / State Supplementary Payment
Social Security Social Security Retirement or Disability benefits
Wages Employment income
Pension Retirement pension income
Other Any other income sources

SOC Linkage Types:

  • Recipient Only: SOC applies only to recipient's income
  • Recipient and Spouse: Combined income of recipient and spouse
  • Family Unit: Household income considered

2 Health Care Certification (SOC 873/874)

â–¼

Legal Requirement - Senate Bill 72 (2011)

IHSS applicants must submit a completed SOC 873 - "Health Care Certification" or alternative documentation (SOC 874) before IHSS services can be authorized.

SOC 873 - Health Care Certification

â–¼

Purpose:

The SOC 873 form must be completed by a Licensed Health Care Professional (LHCP) to certify that the applicant has a medical need for IHSS services.

Licensed Health Care Professionals Include:

  • Physician (MD or DO)
  • Nurse Practitioner (NP)
  • Physician Assistant (PA)
  • Clinical Psychologist
  • Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW)

Required Information on SOC 873:

  • Applicant's medical diagnosis(es)
  • Functional limitations and impairments
  • Certification that applicant needs assistance with activities of daily living
  • LHCP signature, license number, and date

SOC 874 - Alternative Documentation

â–¼

When Used:

Alternative documentation may be accepted instead of SOC 873 in certain circumstances.

Acceptable Alternative Documents:

  • Hospital discharge summary
  • Physician's letter or medical report
  • Recent medical assessment
  • Nursing home discharge papers
  • Other professional medical documentation

Documentation Requirements

Alternative documentation must contain:

  • Medical diagnosis
  • Description of functional limitations
  • Statement of need for assistance
  • LHCP signature and credentials

Health Care Certification Process

â–¼

Timeline:

  1. Day 0: County requests SOC 873 from applicant/LHCP
  2. Day 35: System generates 10-day advance notice task to caseworker
  3. Day 44: System generates 1-day before due date task
  4. Day 45: Due date - SOC 873 must be received
  5. If not received: Case must be denied or terminated

Good Cause Extension

The initial 45-day deadline can be extended for an additional 45 days (total 90 days) for good cause. Extension must be requested before the initial 45-day deadline passes.

Two Exceptions to SOC 873 Requirement:

Exception 1: Hospital Discharge

Services may be authorized when an individual is being discharged from a hospital or health care facility and IHSS is needed to safely return home.

Exception 2: Risk of Out-of-Home Placement

Services may be authorized temporarily when the county determines there is a risk of institutional placement without immediate services.

Exception is Temporary

Even with an exception, the SOC 873 or alternative documentation must still be received within 45 days (or 90 days with good cause extension).

Health Care Certification Operations

â–¼

System Functions:

  • Form Generated: System generates SOC 873 for county to send to LHCP
  • No Form Printed: County obtains form through other means
  • Modify Certification: Update certification information
  • View Certification History: Review all certification records
  • Inactivate Certification: Mark certification as no longer active

Key Fields:

Field Description
Certification Type SOC 873 or SOC 874 (Alternative Documentation)
Request Date Date county requested certification
Due Date 45 days from request date
Good Cause Extension Date Additional 45-day extension if granted
Documentation Received Date Date certification received by county
LHCP Name & License Licensed Health Care Professional information

3 Assessment Types & Processes

â–¼

IHSS assessments determine a recipient's functional abilities, service needs, and authorized hours.

Initial Assessment

â–¼

When Conducted:

First assessment for new IHSS applicants to determine initial eligibility and service authorization.

Assessment Components:

  • Home Visit: Assessor visits applicant's home
  • Functional Assessment: Evaluate ability to perform activities of daily living
  • Environment Assessment: Review home safety and living conditions
  • Task Determination: Identify specific services needed
  • Hours Calculation: Determine assessed need for each task

Required Forms:

  • SOC 293: IHSS Assessment Form (completed by assessor)
  • SOC 873: Health Care Certification (completed by LHCP)

Reassessment

â–¼

When Conducted:

Periodic review (typically annually) to verify continued eligibility and assess any changes in service needs.

Reassessment Triggers:

  • Annual Review: Standard 12-month reassessment
  • Authorization Expiring: Service authorization period ending
  • Policy Requirement: Mandated reassessment due to program changes

Process:

  1. System generates reassessment task
  2. Caseworker schedules home visit
  3. Complete new SOC 293 assessment form
  4. Update functional rankings and service tasks
  5. Determine new authorized hours (may increase, decrease, or stay same)
  6. Generate Notice of Action if hours change

Change Assessment

â–¼

When Conducted:

Unscheduled assessment triggered by reported changes in recipient's condition or circumstances.

Change Assessment Triggers:

  • Medical Change: New diagnosis, hospitalization, surgery
  • Functional Decline: Recipient's abilities have worsened
  • Functional Improvement: Recipient has regained abilities
  • Living Situation Change: Moved to new residence, new household members
  • Recipient Request: Asks for more hours due to increased needs
  • Provider Concern: Provider reports significant changes

Documentation Required:

  • Description of what changed
  • When change occurred
  • Impact on service needs
  • Updated SOC 293 assessment
  • New or updated medical documentation (if applicable)

Assessment Narrative

â–¼

Purpose:

Free-text narrative documenting the assessor's observations, recipient's statements, and justification for service determinations.

Narrative Should Include:

  • Description of recipient's home environment
  • Observations of recipient's functional abilities and limitations
  • Recipient's own description of their needs
  • Safety concerns or environmental hazards
  • Explanation for hours authorized (especially if different from assessed need)
  • Any special circumstances affecting service delivery

Best Practices

Assessment narratives should be detailed, objective, and provide clear justification for service determinations. This documentation is crucial for:

  • State hearing appeals
  • Quality assurance reviews
  • Future reassessments
  • Case audits

4 Service Types & Tasks

â–¼

IHSS services are categorized into three main types: Domestic Services, Related Services, and Personal Care Services.

Domestic Services

â–¼

Service Tasks:

Task Description Typical Frequency
Meal Preparation Planning and cooking meals Daily (1-3 times per day)
Meal Cleanup Washing dishes, cleaning kitchen after meals Daily (1-3 times per day)
Heavy Cleaning Vacuuming, mopping, bathroom cleaning Weekly
Laundry Washing, drying, folding clothes Weekly (1-2 times)
Shopping Grocery and household shopping Weekly or bi-weekly

Domestic Service Limits

Domestic services are limited to tasks directly related to the recipient's needs. Services for other household members are not authorized.

Related Services

â–¼

Yard Hazard Abatement:

Clearing yard hazards that prevent recipient from safely entering/exiting home.

  • Removing debris from walkways
  • Clearing overgrown vegetation blocking paths
  • Removing ice/snow from entryways

Yard Hazard Limitations

Limited to hazards that directly impact recipient's safe entry/exit. General yard maintenance or landscaping is NOT authorized.

Accompaniment:

Provider accompanies recipient to medical appointments, shopping, or other essential activities.

  • Medical Accompaniment: To doctor appointments, therapy, medical procedures
  • Essential Activities: Banking, government offices, essential shopping
  • Not Authorized: Social activities, entertainment, non-essential outings

Personal Care Services

â–¼

Mobility Services:

  • Ambulation: Walking with assistance (cane, walker, physical support)
  • Transfer: Moving between bed/chair/wheelchair/toilet
  • Repositioning: Changing position in bed to prevent bedsores

Hygiene Services:

  • Bathing: Sponge bath, shower, or tub bath assistance
  • Oral Hygiene: Brushing teeth, denture care
  • Grooming: Hair care, shaving, nail care

Bodily Function Services:

  • Bowel/Bladder Care: Toileting assistance, catheter care, colostomy care
  • Menstrual Care: Assistance with menstrual hygiene

Other Personal Care:

  • Dressing: Putting on/taking off clothes, shoes, accessories
  • Feeding: Assistance with eating and drinking
  • Respiration: Assistance with breathing treatments, oxygen equipment

Personal Care Hours

Personal care services typically require more hours than domestic services because they must be provided multiple times daily and require hands-on physical assistance.

Task Details & Proration

â–¼

Single Task vs. Multiple Tasks:

  • Single Task: Only one specific task within a service type (e.g., only meal preparation)
  • Multiple Tasks: Several tasks within a service type (e.g., bathing, dressing, and grooming)

Proration:

When other household members provide some assistance, IHSS hours may be prorated (reduced).

Scenario Proration
No proration 100% of assessed hours authorized
Some household help 75% of assessed hours authorized
Significant household help 50% of assessed hours authorized
Major household help 25% of assessed hours authorized

Proration Documentation

When applying proration, assessors must clearly document:

  • Who in the household is providing help
  • What tasks they help with
  • How often they help
  • Why IHSS hours are still needed despite household help

5 Protective Supervision

â–¼

Protective Supervision is a specialized service for recipients who cannot be left alone due to mental impairment or mental illness.

Eligibility Criteria

Protective Supervision requires ALL of the following:

  1. Recipient has severe mental impairment or mental illness
  2. Recipient cannot be left alone safely
  3. Without supervision, recipient would be at risk of injury or harm to self or others
  4. Mental impairment/illness is the primary reason supervision is needed

Protective Supervision Categories:

Category A

Cannot be left alone at all. Requires constant presence of provider.

Hours: Up to 195 hours per month

Category B

Can be left alone for limited periods. Requires regular check-ins.

Hours: Up to 75 hours per month

Assessment Requirements:

  • Mental Status Exam: Documented by qualified professional
  • Behavioral Examples: Specific incidents demonstrating need
  • Risk Assessment: What dangers exist if left alone
  • Frequency Assessment: How often supervision is needed

Protective Supervision is NOT:

  • Babysitting or companionship
  • For physical disabilities only (use other service categories)
  • For general supervision during personal care tasks
  • Available for children under 18 (they already have supervision requirements)

Documentation Requirements:

  • Mental health diagnosis from qualified professional
  • Detailed description of unsafe behaviors
  • Examples of incidents when left alone
  • Statement from medical/mental health professional supporting need
  • Assessment narrative explaining determination

6 Paramedical Services

â–¼

Paramedical services are medically-related tasks that can be safely performed by a non-medical provider under specific conditions.

Important Limitation

Paramedical services can ONLY be authorized if:

  • Task does not require a licensed medical professional
  • Task can be safely performed by a layperson with training
  • Recipient or responsible person has received appropriate training
  • Licensed health care professional has certified the task is appropriate

Common Paramedical Services:

Service Description Training Required
Injections Administering insulin or other prescribed injections Yes - by RN or MD
Blood Pressure Monitoring Taking and recording blood pressure readings Yes - by RN or MD
Blood Glucose Testing Finger-stick glucose monitoring Yes - by RN or MD
Colostomy Care Changing colostomy bags, cleaning site Yes - by RN or MD
Catheter Care Emptying catheter bags, basic catheter care Yes - by RN or MD
Wound Care Basic wound dressing changes (non-complex) Yes - by RN or MD
Medication Assistance Reminding, opening containers, reading labels Minimal - basic instruction

Paramedical Service Authorization Process:

  1. Physician Order: MD/DO prescribes the specific task
  2. SOC 873 Documentation: LHCP certifies task is appropriate for non-medical person
  3. Training Verification: Document who provided training and when
  4. Provider Agreement: Provider must agree to perform task
  5. Task Details: Document frequency and time required
  6. Authorization: County approves paramedical hours

Paramedical Services NOT Authorized:

  • Tasks requiring licensed nurse or medical professional
  • Sterile procedures
  • Complex wound care
  • Suctioning (except oral suctioning in some cases)
  • Tube feeding (unless specific training and approval)
  • Any task the provider is uncomfortable performing

Hours Calculation:

  • Actual time: Only the time to perform the specific paramedical task
  • Frequency: How many times per day/week task must be done
  • Travel time: NOT included (unless task requires medical appointment accompaniment)
  • Setup/cleanup: Included if necessary for the task

7 Teaching & Demonstration

â–¼

Teaching & Demonstration hours allow providers to teach recipients how to perform tasks independently or with less assistance.

Purpose

Teaching & Demonstration promotes recipient independence by providing additional time for providers to teach and train recipients to perform tasks themselves.

When Teaching & Demonstration is Appropriate:

  • Recipient has potential to learn or relearn tasks
  • Recipient is motivated to become more independent
  • Recipient's condition allows for learning (cognitive ability intact)
  • Task can realistically be learned within reasonable timeframe
  • Provider is willing and able to teach

Common Teaching & Demonstration Services:

Task Category Examples Typical Duration
Domestic Tasks Meal preparation, laundry, cleaning techniques 3-6 months
Personal Care Using assistive devices, transfer techniques, dressing 3-6 months
Mobility Walking with walker, wheelchair use, transfer methods 3-6 months
Paramedical Self-injection, blood glucose monitoring, medication management 1-3 months

Teaching & Demonstration Requirements:

  1. Teaching Plan: Identify specific skills to be taught
  2. Timeframe: Set reasonable goals for learning (usually 3-6 months)
  3. Additional Hours: Authorize extra hours beyond task performance time
  4. Progress Tracking: Document recipient's progress toward independence
  5. Reassessment: Evaluate at end of teaching period

Hours Allocation

Teaching & Demonstration typically adds 10-20% additional time to the base task hours, depending on:

  • Complexity of task being taught
  • Recipient's learning ability
  • Number of tasks being taught simultaneously
  • Progress toward independence

Teaching & Demonstration is NOT:

  • A permanent service category - it's time-limited
  • Appropriate for recipients with severe cognitive impairment who cannot learn
  • For general supervision or companionship
  • A substitute for occupational or physical therapy

Ending Teaching & Demonstration:

Teaching & Demonstration hours should be discontinued when:

  • Recipient has learned the task and can perform independently
  • Teaching period has ended (typically 6 months maximum)
  • Recipient is unable to make progress toward independence
  • Recipient no longer wishes to participate in learning

After Teaching & Demonstration ends, hours revert to standard service hours or recipient performs task independently.

8 Disaster Preparedness & Contacts

â–¼

Disaster Preparedness Planning

â–¼

IHSS recipients with special needs may require additional assistance during disasters or emergencies.

Special Impairments Tracked:

  • Blind or Visually Impaired: Cannot see to navigate during emergency
  • Deaf or Hard of Hearing: Cannot hear emergency warnings
  • Mobility Impaired: Cannot evacuate quickly without assistance
  • Cognitive Impaired: May not understand emergency instructions
  • Medically Fragile: Requires electricity for life-support equipment

Life Support Equipment Needs:

Equipment Type Emergency Concern
Oxygen Concentrator Requires electricity or backup oxygen supply
Ventilator Critical life support - needs backup power
CPAP/BiPAP Sleep apnea treatment - needs power
Feeding Pump Tube feeding equipment - needs power
Suction Machine Airway clearance - needs power
Electric Wheelchair Mobility device - needs charging
Refrigerated Medications Requires refrigeration (insulin, etc.)

Extreme Weather Vulnerabilities:

  • Heat Sensitivity: Medical conditions worsened by heat
  • Cold Sensitivity: Medical conditions worsened by cold
  • No Climate Control: Home lacks heating/cooling

County Responsibilities

Counties use disaster preparedness information to:

  • Identify high-risk recipients during emergencies
  • Coordinate with emergency services
  • Prioritize welfare checks after disasters
  • Arrange emergency services or evacuation assistance

Emergency Contacts

â–¼

Emergency contacts are individuals who can be reached if the recipient cannot be contacted or needs assistance.

Contact Types:

Type Purpose
Primary Emergency Contact First person to call in emergency
Secondary Emergency Contact Alternate contact if primary unavailable
Authorized Representative Person authorized to make decisions on recipient's behalf
Disaster Contact Person to contact during disaster/evacuation
Other Contact Other important contacts (medical providers, family, etc.)

Contact Degree (Relationship):

  • Family Member (specify: parent, sibling, child, spouse, etc.)
  • Friend
  • Neighbor
  • Medical Provider
  • Social Worker
  • Other

Contact Operations:

  • Create Contact: Add new emergency contact
  • Modify Contact: Update contact information
  • View Contact: Review contact details
  • Contact History: Track changes to contact information

Best Practices

  • Keep at least 2 emergency contacts on file
  • Verify contact information is current during each assessment
  • Include alternate phone numbers when possible
  • Document the contact's availability and willingness to help

9 Evidence History & Management

â–¼

Complete tracking and management of all service eligibility evidence with full audit history.

Evidence History Search:

  • Search by Date Range: Find evidence entered within specific dates
  • Search by Evidence Type: Filter by household, service, program, or SOC evidence
  • Search by Assessment Type: Initial, Reassessment, or Change
  • View Complete History: All changes, who made them, and when

Delete Functions:

Delete vs. Inactivate

Most evidence should be inactivated rather than deleted to preserve audit history. Delete functions are used only for correcting data entry errors made immediately.

Evidence Type Delete Allowed? Conditions
Household Member Yes Only if added in error and never saved to case
Service Evidence Limited Can delete current assessment before it's approved
Share of Cost Yes Can delete SOC evidence entries
Income Evidence Yes Can delete income entries
Contacts No Must inactivate instead
Health Care Certification No Must inactivate instead

Audit Trail:

All evidence changes are tracked with:

  • Created By: Username of person who created evidence
  • Created Date: Date and time evidence was created
  • Modified By: Username of person who last modified evidence
  • Modified Date: Date and time of last modification
  • Change Description: What was changed
  • Previous Values: Historical values before changes

Quality Assurance

Complete evidence history enables:

  • Quality review audits
  • State hearing documentation
  • Investigation of case discrepancies
  • Training and coaching opportunities
  • Compliance monitoring

10 Business Rules & Validation

â–¼

Service Eligibility is governed by 50+ business rules that ensure data quality and program compliance.

Key Business Rule Categories:

Assessment Rules

Rules governing when assessments can be conducted and what type is required

Hours Calculation Rules

Automatic calculation of assessed need based on functional rankings and task frequencies

Limits on authorized hours, maximum hours by service type, total hours caps

Required fields, valid date ranges, logical consistency checks

Sample Business Rules:

Rule # Category Description

BR SE 05 Hours Authorized hours cannot exceed assessed need

Error Message System:

Service Eligibility includes 280+ specific error messages to guide users in correcting data entry issues:

  • Critical Errors: Must be corrected before saving (red)
  • Warnings: Should be reviewed but can proceed (yellow)
  • Information Messages: Helpful tips and reminders (blue)

Validation Process

When evidence is entered or modified:

  1. System validates all required fields are completed
  2. System checks for logical consistency (dates, relationships, etc.)
  3. Business rules evaluate data against program requirements
  4. Hours calculations are automatically performed
  5. Warnings or errors are displayed if issues found
  6. User must correct critical errors before proceeding
  7. Upon successful validation, evidence is saved
