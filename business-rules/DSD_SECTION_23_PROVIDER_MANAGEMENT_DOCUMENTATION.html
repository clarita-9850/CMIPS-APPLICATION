1 Provider Enrollment & Lifecycle Management

â–¼

Overview

Complete provider lifecycle management from initial enrollment through assignment, maintenance, and termination.

Create & Enroll Provider

â–¼

Provider Enrollment Process:

Step 1: Person Search

Search for provider in system to avoid duplicate enrollments. Search by SSN, name, or Person ID.

Step 2: Create Provider Person

If not found, create new provider person record with demographic information.

Step 3: Provider Enrollment

Complete enrollment forms including W-4, DE-4, I-9, Provider Enrollment Application.

Step 4: Background Check (CORI)

Criminal Offender Record Information check required before approval.

Step 5: Enrollment Approval

Supervisor reviews and approves or rejects enrollment.

Required Information:

  • Personal Information: Name, address, phone, email, SSN, date of birth
  • Tax Information: W-4 (federal), DE-4 (state) withholding elections
  • I-9 Employment Eligibility: Citizenship/work authorization documentation
  • Direct Deposit: Bank account information for electronic payment
  • Emergency Contact: Person to contact in emergency

Provider Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be at least 18 years old
  • Must have valid Social Security Number
  • Must pass criminal background check
  • Cannot be recipient's legally responsible relative (unless exception applies)
  • Cannot have disqualifying criminal convictions (unless recipient waiver granted)

Provider Types:

Type Description Special Rules
Individual Provider Independent contractor providing services Most common provider type
Live-in Provider Resides in recipient's home Different overtime rules apply
Parent as Provider Parent of minor recipient Requires special approval
Spouse as Provider Spouse of recipient Limited circumstances

â–¼

Assign approved providers to recipient cases with specific hours and start dates.

Assignment Requirements:

  • Provider Status: Must be approved and active
  • Case Status: Case must have approved/authorized hours
  • Assignment Start Date: Cannot be before case authorization
  • Provider Hours: Cannot exceed case authorized hours
  • Relationship Check: System validates provider-recipient relationship

Case Provider Operations:

  • Assign Provider: Add provider to recipient case
  • Create/Modify Hours: Set IHSS hours for provider
  • WPCS Details: Assign Waiver Personal Care Service hours (if applicable)
  • View Provider Hours History: Track all hour changes
  • Leave/Terminate Provider: End provider assignment

Multiple Providers per Case

A recipient case can have multiple assigned providers. Total hours assigned to all providers cannot exceed the case's authorized hours.

Terminate Provider

â–¼

Provider Termination Reasons:

  • Provider Request: Provider no longer wishes to provide services
  • Recipient Request: Recipient no longer wants this provider
  • No Longer Providing Services: Provider stopped working
  • Policy Violation: Provider violated IHSS policies
  • Fraud: Provider committed timesheet fraud or other fraud
  • Failed Background Check: New criminal conviction
  • Deceased: Provider has died

Termination Process:

  1. Leave/Terminate Assignment: End specific case assignments first
  2. Set Termination Date: Last date provider worked
  3. Document Reason: Select termination reason and add notes
  4. Final Timesheet: Ensure final timesheet is submitted/processed

Termination vs. Ineligible

Terminate: Ends provider's work assignments but provider can be re-enrolled in the future.

Ineligible: Provider is permanently barred from IHSS (serious fraud, abuse). Cannot be re-enrolled without state approval.

Criminal Offender Record Information (CORI) background checks are mandatory for all IHSS providers.

  • Re-enrollment: Providers returning after 12+ months inactive
  • Periodic Re-check: Counties may require periodic re-checks

CORI Operations:

  • Create CORI: Initiate background check request
  • View CORI: Review background check results
  • Modify CORI: Update CORI information
  • Inactivate CORI: Close CORI record

Disqualifying Convictions

â–¼

Tier 1 - Permanently Disqualifying (No Waiver):

  • Murder
  • Rape
  • Child molestation
  • Sexual abuse of a minor
  • Kidnapping
  • Human trafficking

Tier 1 Convictions

Providers with Tier 1 convictions can NEVER be approved for IHSS, regardless of how long ago the conviction occurred. No waiver or exception possible.

Tier 2 - Disqualifying (Waiver Possible):

  • Elder abuse
  • Dependent adult abuse
  • Assault with deadly weapon
  • Battery causing serious injury
  • Robbery
  • Burglary of residence
  • Fraud against government program
  • Drug trafficking
  • Theft from elder/dependent adult

Tier 2 Waiver Options:

General Exception

County may grant exception if conviction was 10+ years ago and provider demonstrates rehabilitation

Recipient Waiver

Recipient may waive disqualifying conviction to hire preferred provider

3 Recipient Waiver Process

â–¼

Recipients may waive certain criminal background disqualifications to hire their preferred provider.

Waiver Requirements

  • Only applies to Tier 2 disqualifying convictions
  • Recipient must be fully informed of provider's criminal history
  • Recipient must sign written waiver acknowledging risks
  • County must approve waiver request
  • Waiver is case-specific (applies only to that recipient-provider relationship)

Recipient Waiver Process:

Step 1: Provider Background Check Returns Tier 2 Conviction

Provider enrollment is initially denied due to disqualifying conviction.

Step 2: Recipient Requests Waiver

Recipient indicates desire to hire provider despite criminal history.

Step 3: Criminal History Disclosure

County provides recipient with full details of provider's conviction(s).

Step 4: Recipient Signs Waiver Form

Recipient completes and signs SOC 2298 - Recipient Waiver form, acknowledging they understand the risks.

Step 5: County Reviews Waiver Request

County reviews waiver considering nature of conviction, time elapsed, and recipient's wishes.

Step 6: County Approves or Denies

County makes final determination. If approved, provider can be hired for that specific case only.

Waiver Operations:

  • Create Recipient Waiver: Document waiver request and form
  • View Recipient Waiver: Review waiver details
  • Modify Recipient Waiver: Update waiver information
  • Inactivate Recipient Waiver: End waiver (provider no longer works for recipient)
Waiver Field Description
Waiver Request Date Date recipient requested waiver
Waiver Form Signed Date Date recipient signed SOC 2298 form
County Decision Approved or Denied
County Decision Date Date county made decision
Conviction Details Description of provider's criminal conviction
Waiver Justification Why recipient wants to hire this provider despite conviction

Waiver Scope

Recipient waivers are case-specific. If the provider wants to work for a different recipient, that recipient must also sign a waiver.

4 Provider Travel Time Compensation

â–¼

Providers who work for multiple recipients may be eligible for travel time compensation when traveling directly from one recipient to another.

Travel Time Eligibility

Travel time is compensable when a provider travels directly between two recipient homes during their workday. Travel from home to first recipient or from last recipient to home is NOT compensable.

Travel Time Rules:

  • Between Recipients Only: Must travel directly from Recipient A to Recipient B
  • Same Workday: Both work shifts must be on the same day
  • Reasonable Route: Most direct route between locations
  • No Detours: Cannot claim travel time for personal errands en route
  • County-Approved: Travel time must be pre-approved by county

Travel Time Calculation:

Distance Typical Travel Time
0-5 miles 0.25 hours (15 minutes)
5-10 miles 0.5 hours (30 minutes)
10-20 miles 0.75 hours (45 minutes)
20+ miles 1.0+ hours (based on actual travel time)

Travel Time Operations:

  • Create Travel Time: Set up travel time between two recipients
  • Select Traveling From Recipient: Choose origin recipient
  • Select Traveling To Recipient: Choose destination recipient
  • View Travel Time: Review travel time details
  • Modify Travel Time: Update travel hours or dates
  • Leave/Terminate Travel Time: End travel time authorization
  • Travel Time History: Track all travel time changes

Travel Time & Overtime

Travel time counts toward provider's weekly hours for overtime calculation. If travel time causes provider to exceed 40 hours/week, overtime pay applies.

Example Travel Time Scenario:

Provider Schedule:

  • 8:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Work for Recipient A (4 hours)
  • 12:00 PM - 12:30 PM: Travel from A to B (0.5 hours - compensable)
  • 12:30 PM - 4:30 PM: Work for Recipient B (4 hours)

Total Compensable Hours: 8.5 hours (8 work hours + 0.5 travel hours)

5 Provider Workweek Agreement (SOC 2255)

â–¼

Provider Workweek Agreements define the 7-day workweek period used to calculate overtime for FLSA compliance.

What is a Workweek?

Under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), overtime is calculated based on a fixed 7-consecutive-day workweek. The Provider Workweek Agreement (SOC 2255) establishes which 7 days constitute the provider's workweek.

Workweek Requirements:

  • 7 Consecutive Days: Must be fixed 7-day period (e.g., Sunday-Saturday, Monday-Sunday)
  • Same for All Recipients: Provider uses same workweek for all their recipient cases
  • Cannot Change Frequently: Workweek should remain consistent; changes require new agreement
  • Signed Agreement Required: Provider and recipient(s) must sign SOC 2255 form

Common Workweek Configurations:

Workweek Days When Used
Sunday - Saturday Sun, Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat Most common - matches calendar week
Monday - Sunday Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat, Sun Common for business week alignment
Saturday - Friday Sat, Sun, Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri Less common, but valid

Provider Workweek Agreement Operations:

  • Create Workweek Agreement: Establish workweek definition
  • Select Recipient(s): Apply workweek to all recipients provider works for
  • View Workweek Agreement: Review current workweek
  • Modify Workweek Agreement: Update workweek (requires new signed form)
  • Inactivate Workweek Agreement: End agreement when provider stops working
  • Workweek Agreement History: Track all workweek changes

Importance of Workweek Agreement

Without a properly executed Provider Workweek Agreement, the system cannot accurately calculate overtime. This can result in underpayment to providers and FLSA violations for the county.

Example:

Provider's Workweek: Sunday through Saturday

Hours Worked:

  • Sunday: 6 hours
  • Monday: 8 hours
  • Tuesday: 8 hours
  • Wednesday: 10 hours
  • Thursday: 8 hours
  • Friday: 4 hours
  • Saturday: 0 hours

Total Weekly Hours: 44 hours

Overtime Calculation: 4 hours over 40 hours = 4 hours at time-and-a-half rate

6 Provider Overtime Violations & Tracking

â–¼

Comprehensive tracking system for detecting and managing provider overtime violations to ensure FLSA compliance.

What is an Overtime Violation?

An overtime violation occurs when a provider works more than 40 hours in their defined workweek for a single recipient WITHOUT an approved Recipient Overtime Agreement in place.

Automatic Overtime Detection

â–¼

System Monitoring:

CMIPS automatically detects overtime violations by:

  • Timesheet Review: Analyzes submitted timesheets for hours over 40/week
  • Workweek Calculation: Uses provider's defined workweek period
  • Agreement Check: Verifies if Recipient Overtime Agreement exists
  • Violation Generation: Creates violation record if no agreement found

Violation Detection Triggers:

Scenario Violation? Explanation
45 hours in week, no OT agreement YES Violation - worked over 40 without authorization
45 hours in week, OT agreement in place NO Legal - overtime pre-authorized
50 hours across 2 recipients NO Legal - overtime applies per recipient, not total
35 hours in week NO No violation - under 40 hours

Violation Review Process

â–¼

Multi-level review process for overtime violations with county, state, and CDSS involvement.

Review Levels:

Level 1: County Review

County caseworker reviews violation, determines if legitimate or error.

  • Sustained: Violation is confirmed
  • Not Sustained: Violation was system error or other non-violation
  • County Supervisor Approval: Supervisor must approve county decision

Level 2: County Dispute (Optional)

If county disagrees with state assessment, county can dispute.

  • Dispute Reason: County explains why they disagree
  • Supporting Documentation: County provides evidence
  • County Supervisor Approval: Supervisor must approve dispute

Level 3: State Review

State IHSS office reviews county's determination.

  • Agree with County: Accepts county finding
  • Disagree with County: Overrules county, sends to CDSS
  • State Supervisor Approval: State supervisor must approve

Level 4: CDSS Review (Final)

California Department of Social Services makes final determination.

  • Final Decision: CDSS decision is binding
  • CDSS Supervisor Approval: CDSS supervisor approves final outcome

Violation Outcomes:

Outcome Result
Not Sustained Violation dismissed, no action taken
Warning First offense, provider warned, no penalty
Penalty Provider/recipient may face penalties (payment recovery, case restrictions)
Provider Ineligibility Serious/repeat violations may result in provider being barred from IHSS

Violation Tracking & History

â–¼

Violation Operations:

  • View Overtime Violations: See all violations for provider
  • Violation Details History: Complete timeline of violation
  • Modify County Review: County staff updates review decision
  • Modify County Supervisor Review: Supervisor approves county review
  • Modify County Dispute: County files dispute with state
  • Modify Dispute Supervisor: Supervisor approves dispute
  • Modify State Review: State updates their review
  • Modify State Supervisor Review: State supervisor approval
  • Modify CDSS Review: CDSS enters final determination
  • Modify CDSS Supervisor Review: CDSS supervisor approval

Violation Tracking Report Shows:

  • Violation date and hours worked
  • Recipient case involved
  • County review status and outcome
  • State review status and outcome
  • CDSS final determination
  • Any penalties imposed
  • Pattern of violations (first, second, repeat offender)

7 Overtime Violation Exemptions

â–¼

Extraordinary circumstances may warrant exemption from overtime violation penalties.

Exemption Purpose

Overtime exemptions are granted only in extraordinary circumstances where the overtime violation was unavoidable and necessary for recipient health/safety.

Qualifying Extraordinary Circumstances:

  • Emergency Medical Situation: Recipient had medical emergency requiring extended provider presence
  • Hospitalization: Recipient hospitalized, provider needed for continuity of care
  • Natural Disaster: Earthquake, fire, flood required provider to stay beyond normal hours
  • Sudden Provider Unavailability: Backup provider suddenly unavailable, primary provider had to cover
  • COVID-19 or Pandemic: Pandemic-related circumstances prevented normal staffing

Exemption Request Process:

Step 1: Exemption Request

County or provider requests exemption, explaining extraordinary circumstances.

Step 2: Documentation

Provide supporting documentation (hospital records, emergency reports, etc.).

Step 3: County Review

County reviews circumstances and makes recommendation.

Step 4: State Approval

State IHSS office reviews and approves or denies exemption.

Exemption Operations:

  • Create Overtime Exemption: Request exemption for specific violation
  • View Overtime Exemption: Review exemption details
  • Modify Overtime Exemption: Update exemption information
  • Inactivate Overtime Exemption: Withdraw exemption request
  • Exemption History: Track all exemption requests and outcomes
Exemption Status Description
Requested Exemption request submitted, pending review
Under Review County or state currently reviewing request
Approved Exemption granted, violation excused
Denied Exemption denied, violation stands

Exemption vs. Overtime Agreement

Overtime Exemption: Excuses an overtime violation that already occurred due to extraordinary circumstances.

Recipient Overtime Agreement: Pre-authorizes provider to regularly work over 40 hours/week, preventing violations from occurring.

9 Provider Sick Leave

â–¼

California law requires paid sick leave for IHSS providers.

Sick Leave Accrual

Providers accrue 1 hour of paid sick leave for every 30 hours worked, up to a maximum accrual cap.

Sick Leave Rules:

  • Accrual Rate: 1 hour per 30 hours worked
  • Maximum Accrual: Typically 48 hours (varies by county)
  • Usage Cap: Can use up to 24 hours per year
  • Eligibility: Available after 90 days of employment
  • Carryover: Unused hours carry over to next year (up to cap)

Sick Leave Usage:

Providers can use sick leave for:

  • Provider's own illness or medical appointments
  • Care for sick family member
  • Preventive care (doctor visits, health screenings)
  • Domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking situations

Sick Leave Operations:

  • View Sick Leave Hours: Check provider's current accrued balance
  • View Sick Leave Details: See accrual and usage history
  • Sick Leave on Timesheet: Provider marks hours as sick leave when submitting timesheet
Field Description
Hours Accrued Total sick leave hours earned to date
Hours Used Sick leave hours used to date
Hours Available Current balance available for use
Year-to-Date Used Hours used in current calendar year

Sick Leave Payment

When provider uses sick leave, they are paid at their regular hourly rate. Sick leave hours do not count toward overtime calculation.
