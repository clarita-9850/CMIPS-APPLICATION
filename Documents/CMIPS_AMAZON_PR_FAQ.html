California Launches CMIPS: Revolutionary Cloud-Native Platform Transforms IHSS Management for 58 Counties

New enterprise platform enables zero-configuration deployment, real-time case management, and intelligent compliance tracking for California's largest home care program

Location: Sacramento, CA
Date: December 12, 2025
Classification: Public

SACRAMENTO, CA — December 12, 2025 — The California Department of Social Services (CDSS) today announced the general availability of CMIPS (Case Management and In-Home Supportive Services Platform), a groundbreaking cloud-native solution designed to modernize how California's 58 counties manage the In-Home Supportive Services (IHSS) program. CMIPS replaces legacy systems with a unified, fully configurable platform that streamlines provider enrollment, automates timesheet processing, enforces compliance with state regulations, and delivers real-time visibility into program operations.

"For years, county caseworkers have struggled with disconnected systems, manual processes, and inconsistent enforcement of program rules. CMIPS changes everything. From the moment a provider enrolls to the final payroll approval, every step is tracked, validated, and compliant. Our caseworkers can now focus on what matters most—helping recipients receive the care they need." — Maria Rodriguez, Director of IHSS Modernization at CDSS

The Problem: Fragmented Legacy Systems Burden Counties and Recipients

California's IHSS program serves over 600,000 recipients who receive critical in-home care from more than 500,000 providers. Managing this massive program across 58 counties has historically required navigating a patchwork of legacy mainframe systems, paper-based workflows, and manual compliance checks. Counties faced:

  • Inconsistent eligibility determinations due to manual calculations and varying interpretations of state business rules
  • Overtime violation tracking failures leading to compliance audit findings and improper payments
  • Delayed timesheet processing causing provider payment delays of up to 3 weeks
  • Siloed data preventing cross-county coordination and fraud detection
  • Manual Health Care Certification tracking resulting in missed deadlines and program compliance issues

The Solution: CMIPS Delivers End-to-End Program Management

CMIPS addresses these challenges through a modern, event-driven architecture that automates complex business rules while providing unprecedented flexibility for county-specific configurations.

Key Capabilities:

  1. Zero-Hardcode Configuration — All roles, permissions, field visibility, and workflows are configurable through an administrative UI. Counties can customize CMIPS to their operational needs without code changes or system downtime.
  2. Automated Business Rules Engine — Over 200 IHSS business rules are encoded and enforced automatically, including:
    • SSN verification batch processing with Social Security Administration
    • Provider eligibility determination based on Tier 1/2 crime classifications
    • Overtime violation detection with progressive discipline (1st through 4th violations)
    • Share of Cost (SOC) calculations and tracking
    • Health Care Certification (SOC 873) due date management with automatic task generation
  3. Electronic Visit Verification (EVV) Integration — GPS-based check-in/check-out tracking ensures providers deliver services at authorized locations, with automatic violation detection for time and location discrepancies.
  4. Real-Time Work Queue Management — Intelligent task routing directs timesheet approvals, violation reviews, and eligibility assessments to the appropriate caseworkers and supervisors based on county, role, and workload.
  5. Enterprise Security & Compliance — Multi-layered security includes:
    • Row-Level Security (RLS) for county data isolation
    • Field-level data masking with 6 configurable masking types
    • Comprehensive audit logging for all data access and modifications
    • Integration with AWS KMS and HashiCorp Vault for encryption key management

Customer Quote

"Sacramento County processes over 50,000 timesheets monthly. Before CMIPS, our approval queue backed up for days every pay period," said Jennifer Thompson, Sacramento County IHSS Program Manager. "Now, timesheets flow through automatically. Our caseworkers receive real-time notifications, violations are flagged instantly, and we've reduced our processing time by 60%. Most importantly, our providers get paid on time."

Getting Started

CMIPS is available immediately to all California counties through the CDSS Technology Services Division. Counties can begin deployment in as little as 4 weeks with the standard configuration, or work with the CDSS implementation team to customize workflows, reports, and integrations for their specific operational requirements.

For more information, visit cdss.ca.gov/cmips or contact the CMIPS Implementation Team at cmips-support@cdss.ca.gov.

Section 1: Product Overview

Q1: What is CMIPS?

A: CMIPS (Case Management and In-Home Supportive Services Platform) is an enterprise-grade, cloud-native case management system designed specifically for California's In-Home Supportive Services (IHSS) program. It provides a unified platform for managing:

  • Provider Enrollment & Management — Complete lifecycle from initial application through termination
  • Recipient Services — Case management, eligibility determination, and service authorization
  • Timesheet Processing — Electronic submission, multi-level approval workflows, and payroll integration
  • Electronic Visit Verification (EVV) — GPS-based service delivery verification
  • Compliance Tracking — Automated business rules enforcement and violation management
  • Reporting & Analytics — Real-time dashboards and configurable batch reports

CMIPS replaces legacy mainframe systems with a modern microservices architecture built on Spring Boot, Next.js, and Keycloak for identity management.

Q2: What problem does CMIPS solve?

A: CMIPS addresses five critical challenges facing California's IHSS program:

Q4: What makes CMIPS different from existing case management systems?

A: CMIPS introduces three architectural innovations that distinguish it from legacy systems:

1. Configuration-First Design

Unlike traditional systems that require code changes for customization, CMIPS externalizes all configuration into database tables:

  • Role Definitions — Create custom roles with granular permission sets
  • Field Visibility Rules — Control which fields each role can see/edit
  • Data Masking Policies — Apply 6 masking types (full, partial, hash, random, null, custom) to sensitive fields
  • Workflow Routing — Define approval chains and task assignment logic
  • Business Rule Parameters — Adjust thresholds, time limits, and validation criteria without code deployment

2. Event-Driven Architecture

CMIPS uses Apache Kafka to decouple services and enable asynchronous processing:

  • Event Topics — case-events, timesheet-events, provider-events, task-events
  • Consumer Services — Independent microservices consume events to trigger workflows, send notifications, and update analytics
  • Benefits — Improved scalability, fault tolerance, and the ability to add new functionality without modifying existing services

3. Embedded Business Rules Engine

Over 200 IHSS regulations are encoded as executable rules within the platform:

  • Declarative Rule Definition — Rules written in SQL functions and Java services
  • Real-Time Enforcement — Rules execute at transaction time (e.g., timesheet submission)
  • Audit Trail — Every rule execution is logged with input parameters and results
  • Examples — Overtime calculations, share-of-cost verification, provider eligibility checks

Q6: What database architecture does CMIPS use?

A: CMIPS uses PostgreSQL 14+ with a multi-schema design for data isolation and security:

Schema Purpose Key Tables Access Control
keycloak_schema Identity & access management user_entity, realm, role_mapping Managed by Keycloak (no direct app access)
cmips_application Core business data cases, recipients, providers, timesheets, provider_assignments Row-Level Security based on county/role
cmips_audit Compliance & audit logging audit_log, security_events, access_log Write-only for app, read-only for auditors
cmips_reporting Read-optimized views vw_case_summary, vw_provider_workload, vw_timesheet_summary Read-only access for all users

Key Database Features:

  • Row-Level Security (RLS) — Automatic filtering of data based on user's county and role
  • Column-Level Encryption — SSN and sensitive PII encrypted using pgcrypto
  • Table Partitioning — Timesheets and audit logs partitioned by month for performance
  • Read Replication — Read replicas for reporting queries to offload primary database
  • Connection Pooling — HikariCP with 20-50 connections per service instance

Benefits of Event-Driven Design:

  • Decoupling — Services don't need to know about each other; they only publish/consume events
  • Scalability — Each consumer service can scale independently based on load
  • Reliability — Kafka retains events, allowing failed consumers to retry or replay
  • Auditability — Every event is logged, providing a complete audit trail
  • Extensibility — New functionality can be added by adding new event consumers without modifying existing services

Section 3: Security & Compliance

Q8: How does CMIPS ensure data security?

A: CMIPS implements a defense-in-depth security model with multiple layers of protection:

Layer 1: Network Security

  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all traffic
  • VPC isolation in AWS
  • API Gateway with rate limiting and IP filtering
  • Web Application Firewall (WAF) rules

Layer 2: Authentication & Authorization

  • Keycloak for centralized identity management
  • OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect for secure authentication
  • JWT tokens with 15-minute expiration
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) required for admin roles
  • User-Managed Access (UMA) for fine-grained permissions

Layer 3: Database Security

  • Row-Level Security (RLS) — Automatic data filtering based on user's county and role
  • Column Encryption — SSN, bank account numbers encrypted using pgcrypto
  • Field Masking — 6 masking types (full, partial, hash, random, null, custom)
  • Schema Separation — Logical isolation of data by function

Layer 4: Application Security

  • Input Validation — All user input sanitized to prevent injection attacks
  • CSRF Protection — Tokens required for state-changing operations
  • API Rate Limiting — 100 requests per minute per user
  • Session Management — 15-minute idle timeout, absolute 8-hour limit

Layer 5: Audit & Monitoring

  • Comprehensive Audit Logging — All data access and modifications logged
  • Anomaly Detection — ML-based detection of suspicious access patterns
  • Real-Time Alerts — Notifications for security events (failed logins, privilege escalation attempts)
  • SIEM Integration — Logs exported to Splunk for analysis
Q9: What data masking capabilities does CMIPS provide?

A: CMIPS supports 6 configurable masking types for sensitive fields:

Masking Type Description Example Use Case
FULL Replace entire value with asterisks 123-45-6789 → *********** Complete hiding of highly sensitive data
PARTIAL Show first/last N characters 123-45-6789 → ***-**-6789 SSN, account numbers (show last 4 for verification)
HASH One-way SHA-256 hash john@example.com → a3b8c... Anonymization for analytics
RANDOM Replace with random data of same type 555-1234 → 923-8476 Testing/demo environments
NULL Replace with NULL value john@example.com → NULL Complete data suppression
CUSTOM Apply custom SQL function User-defined transformation Complex business logic (e.g., age ranges)

Configuration: Masking rules are defined in the field_masking_rules table with conditions based on role, county, and data sensitivity level.

Section 4: Configuration & Customization

Q10: How does zero-configuration work in practice?

A: CMIPS externalizes all configuration into database tables, enabling runtime customization without code deployment:

Configuration Type Table What Can Be Configured
Roles & Permissions roles, role_permissions Create custom roles, assign granular permissions (CREATE, READ, UPDATE, DELETE, APPROVE)
Field Visibility field_permissions Control which fields each role can see/edit on each screen
Data Masking field_masking_rules Apply masking types (full, partial, hash, etc.) to sensitive fields by role
Workflow Routing workflow_definitions Define approval chains, escalation rules, and task assignment logic
Business Rules business_rule_configs Adjust thresholds (e.g., overtime limits), time periods, validation criteria
Work Queues work_queue_definitions Create custom queues, assign users, set priority rules

Example: A county wants caseworkers to see only the last 4 digits of provider SSNs. An administrator can:

  1. Navigate to Admin Console → Field Masking Rules
  2. Select field: providers.ssn
  3. Select role: CASEWORKER
  4. Choose masking type: PARTIAL (last 4)
  5. Click "Save"

The change takes effect immediately for all caseworkers—no code deployment required.

Section 7: Business Rules Engine

Q13: What business rules are automated in CMIPS?

A: CMIPS automates over 200 IHSS business rules across multiple domains:

Provider Management (BR PVM Series)

  • BR PVM 03-05: SSN Verification — Batch verification with SSA, fail triggers manual review
  • BR PVM 13: Relationship Impact on Funding — Automatic determination of funding source based on provider-recipient relationship
  • BR PVM 58-76: Workweek Agreement — FLSA-compliant workweek definitions, overtime threshold calculation
  • BR PVM 90-190+: Overtime Violations — Progressive discipline (1st: warning, 2nd: training, 3rd: 90-day suspension, 4th: termination review)

Service Eligibility (BR SE Series)

  • BR SE 01-05: Living Situation Assessment — Determine eligibility based on recipient's living arrangement
  • BR SE 07-08: Higher Than Guidelines (HTG) Indicators — Flag cases requiring additional review
  • BR SE 16-24: Share of Cost (SOC) Calculation — Monthly SOC amount based on recipient income
  • BR SE 28-49: Health Care Certification (HCC) — Track SOC 873 due dates, auto-generate follow-up tasks

Timesheet Processing

  • Maximum 24 hours per day validation
  • Cannot exceed authorized hours per service type
  • Require recipient approval before payment processing
  • EVV compliance verification (time, location, service type)
  • Automatic overtime calculation based on workweek agreement
